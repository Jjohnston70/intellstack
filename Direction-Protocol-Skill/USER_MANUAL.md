## What This Is

Direction Protocol is TNDS's complete sales process for client acquisition. It takes prospects from first contact through signed engagement in five stages:

1. **IDENTIFY** (15-20 min) — Qualify the lead
2. **ASSESS** (30 min) — Validate the problem and fit
3. **MAP** (90-120 min) — Deep diagnostic and pricing
4. **CHART** (30-45 min) — Present findings and proposal
5. **LAUNCH** — Sign, pay, kick off

**Core Message:** "Direction Protocol gets you clarity. Command Protocol gets you control. That's how we turn data into direction."

### When Direction Protocol Applies

**Use Direction Protocol when:**
- Evaluating a new prospect or lead
- Moving a prospect through the sales pipeline
- Conducting discovery or assessment calls
- Mapping client operations and needs
- Creating proposals and quotes
- Analyzing client fit against TNDS ICP

**Don't use Direction Protocol when:**
- Client already signed (use Command Protocol)
- Just need ARO Assessment (use ARO skill)
- Existing client relationship (use appropriate skill)

## The Direction Protocol Workflow (Summary)

```
STAGE 1: IDENTIFY (15-20 min)
├── Ask three core questions
├── Listen for pain signals
├── Check for disqualifiers
├── Calculate qualification score
└── Outcome: Schedule ASSESS or Walk Away

STAGE 2: ASSESS (30 min)
├── Five sections: Reality, State, Stakes, Fit, Budget
├── Calculate SRO score
├── Validate fit and readiness
└── Outcome: Schedule MAP or Pipeline Punks

STAGE 3: MAP (90-120 min)
├── 12 Diagnostic Questions
├── Data inventory
├── Complexity scoring
├── Price calculation
└── Outcome: Pricing, modules, CHART prep

STAGE 4: CHART (30-45 min)
├── Present current state
├── Present findings
├── Present recommendation
├── Quote pricing
├── Paint outcome
└── Outcome: Yes / Not Now / Walk Away

STAGE 5: LAUNCH
├── Send agreement
├── Receive payment
├── Schedule kickoff
└── Outcome: → Command Protocol
```

## Step-by-Step Usage

### IDENTIFY Stage

1. **Open with appropriate greeting** based on lead source
2. **Ask the three core questions:**
   - "Tell me about your operation..."
   - "What keeps you up at night..."
   - "Wave a magic wand..."
3. **Listen for pain signals** (GREEN) and disqualifiers (RED)
4. **Calculate qualification score** (0-8)
5. **Decide:** Score 5+ = ASSESS, Score <5 = Walk Away

### ASSESS Stage

1. **Cover five sections:**
   - Operational Reality (10 min)
   - Current State (8 min)
   - Stakes & Readiness (5 min)
   - Fit Check (3 min)
   - Budget Reality (2 min)
2. **Calculate SRO score** (0-12)
3. **Decide:** Score 6+ = MAP, Score <6 = Pipeline Punks/Walk Away

### MAP Stage

1. **Ask the 12 Diagnostic Questions** (organized in 4 groups)
2. **Complete data inventory**
3. **Explore desired state**
4. **Document constraints**
5. **Calculate complexity score** (5-15)
6. **Determine pricing** with adjustments
7. **Identify Command modules**
8. **Create Context Graph v1.0**
9. **Prepare CHART materials**

### CHART Stage

1. **Present Current State** — "Here's what I heard..."
2. **Present Findings** — Three main issues
3. **Present Recommendation** — Modules and approach
4. **Quote Pricing** — State it, stop talking
5. **Paint Outcome** — The "after" picture
6. **Close** — "Does this make sense?"

### LAUNCH Stage

1. **Send agreement** (same day)
2. **Follow up** (Day 2, 5, 10)
3. **Receive payment** (50% deposit)
4. **Schedule kickoff**
5. **Transition to Command Protocol**

## Integration with ARO Assessment

### When Client Has ARO Assessment

If client completed ARO Assessment before implementation:
1. ARO findings = MAP findings (no re-MAP needed)
2. Apply $500 credit to implementation
3. Jump to CHART stage
4. Context Graph already exists

### When to Recommend ARO During Direction Protocol

If during ASSESS or MAP you hear AI/automation pain:
- "Our AI tools don't work consistently"
- "We want automation but don't know where"
- "ChatGPT doesn't understand our business"

Consider recommending ARO Assessment first.

## Common Patterns

### "Just Give Me a Price" Client

1. Explain why MAP is needed
2. Offer range if they insist
3. Emphasize protection for both parties

Script:
> "I could give you a range, but it would be so wide it wouldn't be useful. The MAP session is how I figure out exactly what you need."

### "Tried Everything" Client

1. Explore what they tried
2. Identify why it failed
3. Explain how this is different

Script:
> "Tell me about that. What happened? Here's how our approach is different..."

### "Need It Yesterday" Client

1. Identify Quick Wins
2. Start immediate progress
3. Build full solution in parallel

Script:
> "We can start on Quick Wins immediately while building the full solution. You'll see progress within [timeframe]."

### "Perfect Fit" Client

1. Don't skip stages
2. Document thoroughly
3. Set clear expectations

Even great fits need the full process to scope correctly.
