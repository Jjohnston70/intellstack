# Part 1: Discovery Framework

## Purpose of Discovery

Discovery happens across three stages:
- **IDENTIFY** — Quick qualification (15-20 min)
- **ASSESS** — Deeper validation (30 min)
- **MAP** — Complete diagnostic (90-120 min)

Each stage has different objectives and questions.

---

## Stage 1: IDENTIFY Questions

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Goal:** Determine if there's enough fit to justify ASSESS

### The Three Core Questions

These are the foundation of every IDENTIFY call:

**1. The Operation Question**
> "Tell me about your operation. What do you do, and how many people are involved?"

*Listen for:*
- Business type and model
- Team size (5-20 is ideal)
- Owner's role and involvement
- Signs of operational chaos

**2. The Pain Question**
> "What's the thing that keeps you up at night about the business right now?"

*Listen for:*
- Specific operational pain (good)
- Vague answers like "growth" (needs probing)
- Wrong problems like "need more marketing" (may not be ICP)

**3. The Magic Wand Question**
> "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing tomorrow, what would it be?"

*Listen for:*
- Specificity (good: "see job costs in real-time")
- Vagueness (bad: "double revenue")
- Operational vs. growth focus

### Follow-Up Probes

| If They Say... | Ask... | Looking For... |
|----------------|--------|----------------|
| "Everything depends on me" | "What happens when you take a day off?" | Owner dependency level |
| "I can't see what's happening" | "Give me an example of finding out too late" | Specific visibility gap |
| "My team doesn't follow processes" | "What processes? How are they documented?" | System maturity |
| "I'm drowning in spreadsheets" | "Walk me through how information flows" | Data chaos level |

### IDENTIFY Qualification Checklist

Before scheduling ASSESS, confirm:

| Factor | Green | Yellow | Red |
|--------|-------|--------|-----|
| **Problem** | Specific operational pain | Vague but real frustration | No real problem |
| **Size** | 5-20 people | 3-4 or 21-30 | <3 or >50 |
| **Decision-maker** | On the call | Has influence | Can't decide anything |
| **Coachable** | Open to questions | Somewhat defensive | Blames everyone else |
| **Ready** | Wants to fix this | Interested but busy | Just exploring |

**Score: 4-5 Green = Schedule ASSESS | 3 Yellow = Proceed with caution | 2+ Red = Walk away**

## Stage 2: ASSESS Questions

**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Goal:** Validate problem is real, prospect is ready to invest, fit is right

### Question Framework: PAIN → IMPACT → CAPACITY → TIMELINE

#### Section 1: Operational Reality (10 min)

**Primary Questions:**
> "Walk me through a typical day or week in your operation."

> "Where in that flow do things usually go wrong?"

> "How do you find out when something's off?"

> "What decisions are you making regularly that you feel like you're guessing on?"

*Listening for:* Chaos signals, manual processes, visibility gaps

#### Section 2: Current State (8 min)

**Primary Questions:**
> "Where does all your business information live right now?"

> "If you needed to know [relevant metric] right now, how would you find out?"

> "Who else in your operation knows how things work, or is it mostly in your head?"

*Listening for:* Data scattered, tribal knowledge, single points of failure

#### Section 3: Stakes and Readiness (5 min)

**Primary Questions:**
> "What's this costing you? Not just money, but time, stress, missed opportunities?"

> "If nothing changes, where are you in a year?"

> "If we fixed this, what would be different?"

*Listening for:* Quantified impact, urgency, vision of better

#### Section 4: Fit Check (3 min)

> "The next step is a 90-minute deep dive called a MAP session. That's where I really understand your operation in detail. Are you open to committing that time?"

*Listening for:* Enthusiasm vs. resistance to deeper engagement

#### Section 5: Budget Reality (2 min)

> "What's your budget range for solving this?"

> (If hesitant) "Have you thought about what fixing this would be worth to you?"

*Listening for:* Realistic expectations vs. price shopping

### SRO Scoring

| Factor | Strong (3-4) | Moderate (2) | Weak (0-1) |
|--------|--------------|--------------|------------|
| **Stakes** | Quantified pain, urgent | Real but not urgent | Theoretical |
| **Readiness** | Ready to act now | Interested, some hesitation | Just exploring |
| **Openness** | Eager for MAP | Willing but cautious | Resistant to process |

**Total 9-10 = Green | 6-8 = Yellow | 1-5 = Red**

## Stage 3: MAP Questions — The 12 Diagnostics

**Duration:** 90-120 minutes
**Goal:** Complete operational understanding to scope and price

### THE BUSINESS (Questions 1-3)

**1. The Identity Question**
> "In one sentence, what does your business do?"

*Look for:* Clarity (they can explain it simply) vs. rambling

**2. The Revenue Question**
> "What are the core activities that produce revenue?"

*Look for:* 2-4 clear revenue drivers

**3. The Team Question**
> "How many people work here? What are the main roles?"

*Look for:* Team structure, who does what, dependencies

### THE FLOW (Questions 4-6)

**4. The Journey Question**
> "Walk me through a typical job from start to finish."

*Look for:* Steps, handoffs, timing, who touches what

**5. The Breakdown Question**
> "Where do things most often go wrong?"

*Look for:* Specific failure points with examples

**6. The Visibility Question**
> "How do you currently know when something is off?"

*Look for:* Early warning systems vs. finding out after the fact

### THE DECISIONS (Questions 7-9)

**7. The Routine Question**
> "What decisions do you make every day/week?"

*Look for:* Recurring decisions that could be systematized

**8. The Friction Question**
> "Which decisions feel hardest or slowest?"

*Look for:* Information gaps, bottlenecks

**9. The Wish Question**
> "What information do you wish you had?"

*Look for:* Missing visibility that would change decisions

### THE BOTTLENECK (Questions 10-12)

**10. The Dependency Question**
> "What happens when you take a day off?"

*Look for:* Owner dependency, single points of failure

**11. The Knowledge Question**
> "Who else knows how things work around here?"

*Look for:* Knowledge distribution, tribal knowledge risks

**12. The Documentation Question**
> "What's in your head that isn't written down?"

*Look for:* Undocumented rules, processes, decisions

### Data Inventory Questions

After the 12 diagnostics, map where data lives:

> "Where does your customer data live?"
> "Where does your financial data live?"
> "Where does project/job information live?"
> "If I needed to know [X], where would I find it?"

**Categories to map:**
- Paper
- Spreadsheets
- Texts/Messages
- Email
- Software/Apps
- Whiteboards
- Someone's head

# Part 2: CHART Presentation Skills

## CHART Structure

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Goal:** Present findings, recommend solution, get commitment

### The Flow

```
1. PRESENT CURRENT STATE (5-7 min)
   ↓
2. PRESENT FINDINGS (5-7 min)
   ↓
3. PRESENT RECOMMENDATION (10-15 min)
   ↓
4. QUOTE & PRICING (5 min)
   ↓
5. THE OUTCOME (5 min)
   ↓
6. CLOSE & NEXT STEPS (5 min)
```

### Section 1: Present Current State

**Purpose:** Show you understand their business

**Script Template:**
> "Here's what I heard and what I see in your operation..."
>
> "You're running [business description] with [X] people. Your main workflows are [workflow 1, 2, 3]. Information lives in [places — spreadsheets, texts, etc.]."
>
> "The main challenge is [their primary pain in their words]."
>
> "Does that capture it correctly?"

**Tips:**
- Use THEIR words, not yours
- Show the data flow diagram
- Validate before moving on

### Section 2: Present Findings

**Purpose:** Show what's broken and why

**Script Template:**
> "Based on my deep dive, here are the three biggest issues:"
>
> "**First:** [Finding 1]. When [situation], [consequence]. This is costing you [quantified impact]. Example: [specific example from MAP]."
>
> "**Second:** [Finding 2]. [Same structure]."
>
> "**Third:** [Finding 3]. [Same structure]."

**Tips:**
- Lead with impact, not features
- Use specific examples they shared
- Tie to money, time, or stress

### Section 3: Present Recommendation

**Purpose:** Show the solution

**Script Template:**
> "Based on what we found, here's what I recommend..."
>
> "We'd install [modules] to give you [capability]."
>
> "[Module 1] handles [problem 1] by [how it works]."
> "[Module 2] handles [problem 2] by [how it works]."
>
> "Implementation takes [X] weeks. Here's the timeline: [milestones]."

**Tips:**
- Frame in terms of their problems, not our features
- Explain what changes for them day-to-day
- Be specific about what they get

### Section 4: Quote & Pricing

**Purpose:** State the investment and stop talking

**Script Template:**
> "The investment for this is $[amount]."
>
> "That includes [brief scope summary]. Timeline is [X] weeks. Payment is [terms]."

**THEN STOP. Wait for their reaction.**

**Tips:**
- State the number confidently
- Don't apologize or justify immediately
- Let silence work

### Section 5: The Outcome

**Purpose:** Paint the "after" picture

**Script Template:**
> "If we do this, here's what changes..."
>
> "Instead of [current pain], you'll be able to [new capability]."
>
> "You'll finally have [visibility/control/time] that you don't have now."
>
> "[Specific outcome they said they wanted in MAP]."

**Tips:**
- Reference their specific goals from discovery
- Quantify where possible
- Make it emotional (freedom, relief, control)

### Section 6: Close & Next Steps

**Purpose:** Get a decision or a deadline

**Script:**
> "Does this make sense for you?"

**Wait for response. Then:**

| If They Say... | You Say... |
|----------------|------------|
| "Yes, let's do it" | "Great. I'll send the agreement today. Once signed with the deposit, we'll get kickoff scheduled." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Of course. What specifically would you like to think through?" |
| "That's more than expected" | "What were you expecting? Let me understand the gap." |
| "I need to talk to [someone]" | "What questions do you think they'll have? Let's address those now." |

# Part 3: Objection Handling — LAER Method

## The Framework

**L** — Listen (don't interrupt)
**A** — Acknowledge ("I understand...")
**E** — Explore (ask a question)
**R** — Respond (address the specific concern)

---

## The 10 Common Objections

### 1. "That's more than I expected" (Price)

**What they mean:** I need to justify this expense

**LAER Response:**
> "I understand. [A] Let me ask — when you said [pain point] was costing you [X hours/dollars], was that accurate? [E]"
>
> *[Wait for confirmation]*
>
> "So if fixing this saves you even half of that, it pays for itself in [timeframe]. Does that math work? [R]"

**If they push back:**
> "What number would feel more comfortable? I want to understand where the gap is."

---

### 2. "I need to think about it" (Stall)

**What they mean:** I'm not ready to decide / need validation

**LAER Response:**
> "Of course. [A] What specifically would you like to think through? [E]"
>
> *[Address their specific concern]*
>
> "When would be a good time to reconnect? I'll follow up then. [R]"

**Probing question:**
> "Is this a 'yes, but I need time' or is there something specific holding you back?"

---

### 3. "Just quote me the automation" (Skip Process)

**What they mean:** I don't want to do the diagnostic

**LAER Response:**
> "I understand you want to move fast. [A] Can I explain why we don't do that? [E]"
>
> "What people think they need and what actually fixes the problem are often different. I've seen businesses waste thousands building the wrong thing. The MAP session protects both of us. [R]"
>
> "It's 90 minutes of your time. Is that really the bottleneck?"

---

### 4. "I've tried stuff like this before" (Skepticism)

**What they mean:** I've been burned / don't trust consultants

**LAER Response:**
> "I've heard that a lot. [A] What happened with the last one? [E]"
>
> *[Listen to their story]*
>
> "That sounds frustrating. Here's how this is different: [specific differentiation based on their concern]. [R]"

**Common differentiators:**
- Fixed scope, fixed price (vs. open-ended)
- Actionable deliverables (vs. shelf-ware)
- We build it, not just advise (vs. consulting)

---

### 5. "I need to talk to my partner/spouse" (Authority)

**What they mean:** Either not the decision-maker OR need validation

**LAER Response:**
> "Totally fair. [A] What questions do you think they'll have? [E]"
>
> *[If they list questions, answer them now]*
>
> "Would it help if I sent a summary you can share? When are you meeting with them? Let's schedule a follow-up after that. [R]"

**Clarifying question:**
> "Is this a 'yes' from you pending their approval, or are you still deciding yourself?"

---

### 6. "Can you guarantee results?" (Risk Aversion)

**What they mean:** I need certainty before investing

**LAER Response:**
> "I understand wanting certainty. [A] What specific result would you want guaranteed? [E]"
>
> *[Understand the specific outcome]*
>
> "I can't guarantee outcomes I haven't diagnosed yet. What I CAN guarantee is that you'll know exactly what needs to change and have a clear plan to do it. Most clients see [typical outcome]. [R]"

**Alternative:**
> "The guarantee is in our process — we don't build until we've diagnosed. That's why the MAP session exists."

---

### 7. "Can you do it faster?" (Urgency)

**What they mean:** I have a deadline / want results now

**LAER Response:**
> "I hear the urgency. [A] What's driving the timeline? [E]"
>
> *[Understand the constraint]*
>
> "We can start on Quick Wins immediately while building the full solution. You'll see progress within [timeframe]. [R]"

**If truly urgent:**
> "For rush timelines, we charge a premium. Would that work for your situation?"

---

### 8. "I found something cheaper" (Competition)

**What they mean:** I'm price shopping / have alternatives

**LAER Response:**
> "Good to know. [A] What specifically are you comparing? [E]"
>
> *[Understand the alternative]*
>
> "Cheaper doesn't always mean better fit. [Explain differentiation]. What matters more to you — price or [outcome]? [R]"

**Key differentiators:**
- Fixed scope, fixed price (vs. hourly)
- Operational focus (vs. tech/dev focus)
- Owner of small business (vs. agency/consultant)

---

### 9. "We can probably do this ourselves" (DIY)

**What they mean:** I don't see the value of paying someone

**LAER Response:**
> "You absolutely could. [A] How many hours would it take you? [E]"
>
> *[Let them estimate]*
>
> "At your hourly rate, is that more or less than the investment? And that's assuming you get it right the first time. [R]"

**Alternative:**
> "Most DIY clients can handle 60-70% themselves. The question is whether the 30-40% that's hard is worth your time to figure out."

---

### 10. "Business is slow right now" (Timing)

**What they mean:** Budget is tight / not a priority

**LAER Response:**
> "I understand. [A] When do you expect things to pick back up? [E]"
>
> *[Get a timeframe]*
>
> "Would it make sense to do the diagnostic now and implement when things improve? That way you're ready to go when budget frees up. [R]"

**Alternative:**
> "Slow periods are actually the best time to fix operational issues. You have bandwidth now that you won't have when things get busy."

# Part 5: Common Scenarios

## Scenario 1: "Just Give Me a Price"

**Situation:** Prospect wants pricing before MAP

**How to Handle:**
> "I hear you want to know what you're looking at. Here's the challenge: I could give you a range, but it would be so wide it wouldn't be useful. Anywhere from $2,500 to $5,000+ depending on what we find."
>
> "The MAP session is how I figure out which end of that range you're at — and what specifically you'd get for it. It's 90 minutes of your time. Worth it to avoid surprises?"

**If they insist:**
> "Based on what you've told me, you're probably in the $[X] range. But that could change significantly once I see your actual systems. Want to lock in a more accurate number?"

---

## Scenario 2: "No Decision-Maker on Call"

**Situation:** The person you're talking to can't make decisions

**How to Handle:**
> "Thanks for your time today. Before we go further, I want to make sure we're setting this up for success."
>
> "Who makes the final call on something like this? Would it make sense to include them in our next conversation?"

**If they want to continue:**
> "Happy to walk you through everything. But I'd recommend we schedule a follow-up with [decision-maker] before I do the full proposal. That way I can address their questions directly."

---

## Scenario 3: "Tech-Resistant Client"

**Situation:** They're skeptical of technology/automation

**How to Handle:**
> "I hear some hesitation about the tech side. Can I ask what concerns you?"
>
> *[Listen]*
>
> "Here's my philosophy: technology should eliminate the work you hate, not replace the work you love. We're not automating your relationships or your judgment. We're automating the admin that's drowning you."
>
> "You stay in control. The systems just make sure nothing falls through the cracks."

---

## Scenario 4: "Budget Constraint"

**Situation:** They want what you offer but can't afford it

**How to Handle:**
> "I appreciate you being honest about budget. A few options:"
>
> "**Option 1:** Pipeline Punks — our DIY program. Lower investment, you do the work, we provide the training. If you later want us to take over, you get 50% credit."
>
> "**Option 2:** Phased approach — we start with the highest-impact item and add the rest over time."
>
> "**Option 3:** Push the timeline — do the diagnostic now, implement when budget frees up."
>
> "Which of those resonates?"

---

## Scenario 5: "Competitive Situation"

**Situation:** They're talking to other providers

**How to Handle:**
> "Good — you should explore options. Can I ask who else you're talking to?"
>
> *[Listen]*
>
> "What specifically are you comparing?"
>
> *[Address the specific comparison point]*
>
> "Here's what makes us different: [specific differentiator]. What matters more to you — [their priority] or [the competitor's strength]?"

**Key differentiators to emphasize:**
- Owner-operated, not agency
- Fixed scope/price, not hourly
- Operational focus, not just tech
- We build it, not just advise
