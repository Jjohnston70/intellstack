## Overview

This document maps the complete Direction Protocol journey from first contact through signed engagement and Command Protocol handoff. It provides the sales framework for all TNDS client acquisition.

**The Transformation:** Direction Protocol gets you clarity. Command Protocol gets you control. That's how we turn data into direction.

**The Two Protocols:**
- **Direction Protocol (Sales)**: How we identify what clients need
- **Command Protocol (Delivery)**: How we build what clients need

## STAGE 0: Lead Generation

**Purpose:** Identify potential TNDS clients from various sources.

### Lead Sources

| Source | Description | Typical Quality |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Referral | Existing client or partner referral | Highest |
| Inbound | Website contact, content response | High |
| Networking | Event, meeting, introduction | Medium-High |
| Outbound | Cold outreach response | Medium |

### Initial Qualifiers

Before moving to IDENTIFY, check:

- [ ] Business has operational pain (not just "want automation")
- [ ] Size appears to be 5-20 employees
- [ ] Owner or decision-maker is accessible
- [ ] Not obviously wrong fit (startup, enterprise, just wants an app)

### Lead Signals That Indicate Direction Protocol Fit

**Strong Signals (Proceed to IDENTIFY):**
- "I can't see what's happening in my business"
- "I find out about problems too late"
- "Everything depends on me"
- "I can't take a day off"
- "My guys don't do things consistently"
- "I'm drowning in spreadsheets / paper / texts"

**Weak Signals (May need different approach):**
- "I just need a specific app built" → Not ICP
- "We need enterprise software" → Not ICP
- "We're a startup figuring things out" → Too early
- "What's the cheapest option?" → Not ready

## STAGE 1: IDENTIFY (15-20 minutes)

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Purpose:** Determine if there's enough fit to justify a longer conversation
**Outcome:** Schedule ASSESS OR polite exit

### Entry Point Openings

**Warm Referral:**
> "Hey [Name], [Referrer] mentioned we should connect. They said you were dealing with [pain point]. What's going on with that?"

**Inbound from Website:**
> "Thanks for reaching out. I saw you mentioned [topic from form]. Tell me more about what prompted you to reach out now."

**Networking Event Follow-up:**
> "Good connecting at [event]. You mentioned [problem]. I wanted to follow up on that conversation."

**Cold Outreach Response:**
> "Appreciate you taking my call. I work with [industry] businesses around [5-20 people] who are dealing with [common pain]. Does that resonate at all?"

### The Three Discovery Questions

Ask these to understand their situation:

1. **"Tell me about your operation. What do you do, and how many people are involved?"**
   - Listen for: Size, complexity, owner involvement
   - Good: 5-20 people, owner drowning
   - Bad: Too small (<5) or too large (20+)

2. **"What's the thing that keeps you up at night about the business right now?"**
   - Listen for: Operational pain, visibility issues
   - Good: "I find out about problems too late"
   - Bad: "I need more marketing" (wrong problem)

3. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing tomorrow, what would it be?"**
   - Listen for: Specific operational issue
   - Good: "See what my team is doing in real-time"
   - Bad: "Double my revenue" (too vague)

### Pain Signals to Listen For

**Qualified Pain (Proceed):**
- [ ] "I can't see what's happening"
- [ ] "I find out about problems too late"
- [ ] "Everything depends on me"
- [ ] "I can't take a day off"
- [ ] "My guys don't do things consistently"
- [ ] "I'm drowning in spreadsheets / paper / texts"

**Disqualifiers (Exit Gracefully):**
- [ ] "I just need someone to build me an app"
- [ ] "I want AI to handle everything"
- [ ] "What's the cheapest option?"
- [ ] "I don't really have a problem, just exploring"
- [ ] Won't commit to a longer conversation
- [ ] Argues about needing to understand before quoting

### Qualification Scoring

| Factor | Yes (2pts) | Maybe (1pt) | No (0pts) |
|--------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| Real operational problem | | | |
| Decision-maker present | | | |
| Sounds coachable | | | |
| Right size (5-20 people) | | | |

**Score Interpretation:**
- 7-8 points: GREEN → Schedule ASSESS
- 5-6 points: YELLOW → Schedule ASSESS with notes
- 0-4 points: RED → Pipeline Punks or Walk Away

### IDENTIFY Outcomes

**IF QUALIFIED (GREEN/YELLOW):**

1. Set expectation for ASSESS stage:
> "It sounds like there's enough here to dig deeper. The next step is a 30-minute call where I'll ask more detailed questions about your operation. That helps me understand if and how we can help. Can you do [day/time]?"

2. CRM: Create deal "Direction Protocol - IDENTIFY Complete"

3. Send calendar invite immediately

**IF NOT QUALIFIED (RED):**

Script:
> "I've really appreciated learning about your business. Based on what I'm hearing, I don't think I'm the right fit for what you need right now. [Optional: brief, honest reason.] I'd rather be upfront about that than waste your time."

**IF BUDGET CONSCIOUS:**

Mention Pipeline Punks:
> "If you're looking for a DIY approach, we have training programs that might be a better fit. I can send you information on Pipeline Punks if you're interested."

## STAGE 2: ASSESS (30 minutes)

**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Purpose:** Validate problem is real, prospect is ready to invest, fit is right
**Outcome:** Schedule MAP OR Pipeline Punks OR polite exit

### Pre-Call Preparation (5-10 min)

- [ ] Review IDENTIFY notes
- [ ] Review any information from website/LinkedIn
- [ ] Prepare specific questions based on their pain points
- [ ] Have note-taking ready

### ASSESS Call Structure

#### Opening (2 minutes)

Script:
> "Thanks for making time for this. Last time we talked, you mentioned [main pain point]. Today I want to dig deeper into that to see if there's a real fit. I'll ask some pointed questions about your operation. Ready to go?"

#### Section 1: Operational Reality (10 minutes)

**Questions:**
- "Walk me through a typical day or week in your operation"
- "Where in that flow do things usually go wrong?"
- "How do you find out when something's off?"
- "What decisions are you making regularly that you feel like you're guessing on?"

*Listen for: Chaos indicators, visibility gaps, owner dependency*

#### Section 2: Current State (8 minutes)

**Questions:**
- "Where does all your business information live right now?"
- "If you needed to know [relevant metric] right now, how would you find out?"
- "Who else in your operation knows how things work, or is it mostly in your head?"

*Listen for: Data scattered, tribal knowledge, single points of failure*

#### Section 3: Stakes and Readiness (5 minutes)

**Questions:**
- "What's this costing you? Not just money, but time, stress, missed opportunities?"
- "If nothing changes, where are you in a year?"
- "If we fixed this, what would be different?"

*Listen for: Quantifiable impact, urgency, vision of better*

#### Section 4: Fit Check (3 minutes)

**Explain MAP stage:**
> "If we move forward, the next step is a 90-minute deep dive called a MAP session. That's where I really understand your operation in detail - workflows, data sources, decision points. That's what lets me give you accurate pricing and a specific solution. Are you open to committing that time?"

*Listen for: Enthusiasm vs. resistance*

#### Section 5: Budget Reality (2 minutes)

**Questions:**
- "What's your budget range for solving this?"
- If hesitant: "Have you thought about what fixing this would be worth to you?"

*Listen for: Realistic expectations vs. price shopping*

### SRO Assessment Framework

| Factor | Strong (3-4) | Moderate (2) | Weak (0-1) |
|--------|--------------|--------------|------------|
| **Stakes** - How painful is this problem? | | | |
| **Readiness** - Are they ready to act now? | | | |
| **Openness** - Will they engage with the process? | | | |

**SRO Total:**
- 9-10: STRONG → Schedule MAP with high confidence
- 6-8: MODERATE → Schedule MAP with concerns noted
- 1-5: WEAK → Pipeline Punks or Walk Away

### Listen For During ASSESS

**Enthusiasm (GREEN):**
- "Yes, I need someone to actually understand my business"
- "When can we schedule the MAP session?"
- "This is exactly what I've been looking for"

**Resistance (YELLOW):**
- "Can't you just tell me what it costs?"
- "90 minutes seems like a lot"
- "I need to think about it"

**Skepticism (RED):**
- "I've tried stuff before and it didn't work"
- "Everyone promises results"
- "What guarantee do you offer?"

### ASSESS Outcomes

**GREEN LIGHT (SRO 9-10):**

Schedule MAP session:
> "Great. Let's schedule the MAP session. I'll need 90 minutes of your focused time. It's diagnostic, not sales - I'm trying to understand your operation deeply enough to give you an accurate solution. Can you do [day/time]?"

- [ ] Schedule MAP session (90-120 min block)
- [ ] Update CRM: Move deal to "Direction Protocol - MAP Scheduled"
- [ ] Send calendar invite with MAP prep instructions
- [ ] Create prospect folder: `20_PROSPECTS/[Client-Name]/`

**YELLOW LIGHT (SRO 6-8):**

Address concerns, then decide:
> "I hear some hesitation. What's holding you back? [Listen] Here's the thing - the MAP session protects both of us. I don't want to quote something that doesn't fit, and you don't want to buy something that won't work."

If they commit → Schedule MAP
If still hesitant → Offer Pipeline Punks:
> "If you want to explore this at your own pace first, we have training programs through Pipeline Punks. You can DIY the diagnostic work and come back to us when you're ready."

**RED LIGHT (SRO 1-5):**

Walk away gracefully:
> "Based on what I'm hearing, I don't think we're the right fit right now. [Specific reason - timing, budget, readiness.] I'd rather be honest than waste your time."

## STAGE 3: MAP (90-120 minutes)

**Duration:** 90-120 minutes
**Purpose:** Deep operational diagnostic to scope solution and pricing
**Outcome:** Pricing calculated, solution defined, CHART presentation ready

### Pre-MAP Preparation (30 min)

**Review all previous notes:**
- [ ] IDENTIFY notes reviewed
- [ ] ASSESS notes reviewed
- [ ] Any additional research done
- [ ] 12 Diagnostic Questions ready
- [ ] Note-taking system ready

**Claude Prep Prompt:**
```
Here are my notes from IDENTIFY and ASSESS with [Client]:
[Paste notes]

Prepare me for the MAP session:
- What should I dig into based on what we've learned?
- What gaps do I need to fill?
- What concerns should I address?
- What Command modules might be relevant?
```

### MAP Session Structure

#### Introduction (5 minutes)

Script:
> "Thanks for making time for this deep dive. Today is diagnostic, not sales. I'm going to ask a lot of detailed questions about how your operation actually works. My goal is to understand well enough that I can tell you exactly what needs to change and what it would cost to fix it. Let's get started."

Set expectations:
- This is exploratory, not a pitch
- Capture real information, not what they think you want to hear
- Will take full 90 minutes
- Next step is CHART presentation with findings and pricing

#### The 12 Diagnostic Questions (45 minutes)

**THE BUSINESS:**

1. **"In one sentence, what does your business do?"**
   - Look for: Clarity vs. rambling
   - Note: Core value proposition

2. **"What are the core activities that produce revenue?"**
   - Look for: 2-4 key activities
   - Note: Revenue drivers

3. **"How many people work here? What are the main roles?"**
   - Look for: Team structure, dependencies
   - Note: Organizational chart sketch

**THE FLOW:**

4. **"Walk me through a typical job from start to finish."**
   - Look for: Steps, handoffs, timing
   - Note: Process map

5. **"Where do things most often go wrong?"**
   - Look for: Specific breakdown points
   - Note: Pain points with context

6. **"How do you currently know when something is off?"**
   - Look for: Early warning vs. after-the-fact
   - Note: Visibility gaps

**THE DECISIONS:**

7. **"What decisions do you make every day/week?"**
   - Look for: Recurring decisions
   - Note: Decision inventory

8. **"Which decisions feel hardest or slowest?"**
   - Look for: Information gaps, bottlenecks
   - Note: Decision friction

9. **"What information do you wish you had?"**
   - Look for: Missing visibility
   - Note: Data needs

**THE BOTTLENECK:**

10. **"What happens when you take a day off?"**
    - Look for: Owner dependency
    - Note: Single points of failure

11. **"Who else knows how things work around here?"**
    - Look for: Knowledge distribution
    - Note: Tribal knowledge risks

12. **"What's in your head that isn't written down?"**
    - Look for: Undocumented rules
    - Note: Documentation gaps

#### Data Inventory (15 minutes)

Document where their information lives:

| Data Type | Location | Access | Format | Issues |
|-----------|----------|--------|--------|--------|
| Customer info | | | | |
| Financial data | | | | |
| Project/job data | | | | |
| Team communication | | | | |
| Documents | | | | |

**Categories to check:**
- Paper
- Spreadsheets
- Texts/Messages
- Email
- Software/Apps
- Whiteboards
- Someone's head

#### Desired State Exploration (15 minutes)

**Questions:**
- "What would 'fixed' look like?"
- "What decisions would you make differently with better visibility?"
- "What would you be able to do that you can't do now?"
- "If I came back in 6 months and everything worked, what would be different?"

*Capture specific outcomes they want*

#### Solution Exploration (15 minutes)

Based on findings, discuss potential Command modules:
- What tools/systems would address the gaps?
- What integrations are needed?
- What would implementation look like?
- What would their involvement need to be?

#### Constraints and Requirements (10 minutes)

**Questions:**
- Budget constraints
- Timeline expectations
- Team involvement available
- Technical constraints or preferences
- Any non-negotiables

#### Wrap and Next Steps (5 minutes)

Script:
> "This has been incredibly helpful. I have a clear picture of what's happening and what needs to change. Here's what happens next: I'm going to take everything we discussed and put together a specific recommendation with pricing. That's the CHART presentation. We'll schedule that for [day/time] - takes about 45 minutes. I'll walk you through what I found and what I recommend. Sound good?"

- [ ] Schedule CHART presentation (30-45 min)
- [ ] Confirm pricing timeline

## STAGE 4: CHART (30-45 minutes)

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Purpose:** Present findings, recommend solution, quote price, get decision
**Outcome:** Client says YES / Negotiate / Not Now / Walk Away

### Pre-CHART Preparation

**Required Deliverables:**
- [ ] One-page current state summary
- [ ] Data flow diagram (visual)
- [ ] Findings list (gaps, issues, pain points)
- [ ] Recommended Command modules
- [ ] Pricing breakdown
- [ ] Service agreement (ready to send)

**Claude Prep Prompt:**
```
Based on the MAP findings for [Client]:
[Paste findings]

Prepare the CHART presentation:
- What 2-3 findings should I emphasize most?
- What objections might arise?
- What's the strongest ROI argument?
- How should I present the pricing?
```

### CHART Presentation Structure

#### 1. Present Current State (5-7 min)

Script:
> "Here's what I heard and what I see in your operation..."

- Recap their situation using their words
- Show data flow diagram
- Highlight where things break down
- Validate understanding: "Does this capture it correctly?"

#### 2. Present Findings (5-7 min)

Walk through key findings:
1. Finding 1: What's happening → What it costs
2. Finding 2: What's happening → What it costs
3. Finding 3: What's happening → What it costs

Use their specific examples from MAP session.

#### 3. Present Recommendation (10-15 min)

Script:
> "Based on what we found, here's what I recommend..."

- Explain proposed solution approach
- Detail specific Command modules
- Explain what each module does for them
- Show how it addresses their specific pain points
- Explain implementation approach and timeline

#### 4. Quote & Pricing (5 min)

Script:
> "The investment for this is $[amount]."

Present pricing:
- Total price
- What's included
- Timeline (2-4 weeks typical)
- Payment terms (50/50 or 40/30/30)

**Then stop talking.** Let them respond.

#### 5. The Outcome (5 min)

After they've absorbed the price:

Script:
> "If we do this, here's what changes..."

Paint the "after" picture:
- What they'll be able to see
- What decisions become easier
- What time they get back
- What stress goes away

Quantify impact where possible.

#### 6. Close and Next Steps (5 min)

Ask directly:
> "Does this make sense for you?"

Listen for their response and handle accordingly.

### Response Handling

| Response | What to Do |
|----------|------------|
| "That sounds good" | → Move to LAUNCH |
| "I need to think about it" | → Ask what questions would help them decide |
| "That's more than I expected" | → Ask what they were expecting, use ROI math |
| "I'm not ready right now" | → Ask what's holding them back, understand timeline |
| "Can you do it for less?" | → Understand what's driving the ask, adjust scope if needed |
| "I need to talk to [partner/spouse]" | → Offer to schedule follow-up call with them present |

### Objection Handling Reference

**"Can't you just tell me the price?" (if asked before)**
> "I could give you a range, but it would be so wide it wouldn't be useful. The MAP stage is how I figure out what Command modules you actually need."

**"Why did I need a MAP session?"**
> "What people think they need and what actually fixes the problem are often different. The MAP stage protects both of us - I don't quote wrong, you don't buy wrong."

**"That's expensive" (Use ROI Math)**
> "Let me put it in perspective. You mentioned [pain point] costs you about [X hours/week]. At your hourly rate, that's $[amount/month]. This investment pays back in [X months]. After that, it's pure return."

Formula:
```
Monthly time saved × hourly value = monthly savings
Investment ÷ monthly savings = payback period

Example: 8 hrs/month × $125/hr = $1,000/month savings
$4,000 ÷ $1,000 = 4 month payback
```

**"Can we start smaller?"**
> "I can't do half a Command Center - it won't solve the problem. But if there's a specific first step that would give you confidence, I'm open to discussing it."

**"Found something cheaper"**
> "Cheaper doesn't mean it solves the problem. What specifically are you comparing? [Listen] The difference is [explain differentiation]."

**"I need to think about it"**
> "Totally fair. What specific questions would help you decide? What's missing that would give you confidence?"

### CHART Outcomes

**IF YES:**
> "Great. I'll send over the service agreement today. Once signed with the 50% deposit, we'll get your kickoff scheduled within the week."

→ Move to LAUNCH stage

**IF NEGOTIATE:**
- Understand the concern (budget? scope? timing?)
- Adjust scope if needed, not just price
- Present modified option
- Get commitment or set decision deadline

**IF NOT NOW:**
> "I understand. When would be a good time to revisit this? I'll set a reminder and check in then. Everything we discussed stays valid."

- Note reason for delay
- Set follow-up date
- Move to Nurture sequence

**IF WALK AWAY:**
> "I appreciate you being honest. If things change, you know where to find me."

- Document reason
- Archive appropriately

| Section | Include |
|---------|---------|
| Scope | Specific Command modules, deliverables |
| Investment | Total price, payment schedule |
| Timeline | Start date, milestone dates, completion estimate |
| Terms | What's included, what's not, revision policy |

- **Day 5:** "Are you still planning to move forward? Any questions I can answer?"

Then stop. Don't chase.

## Post-CHART Paths

### PATH A: Client Says YES

See LAUNCH stage above.

**Summary:**
1. Agreement sent same day
2. Signed and paid within 7 days
3. Kickoff within 14 days
4. → Command Protocol begins

### PATH B: Client Says "Not Now"

**Goal:** Keep relationship warm for future engagement

**Same Day Actions:**
- [ ] Ask: "When should we revisit? What would need to change?"
- [ ] Note reason for delay in CRM
- [ ] Send "Not Now" follow-up email
- [ ] Update CRM: Mark deal "Direction Protocol - Nurture"

**File Management:**
- [ ] Move folder: `20_PROSPECTS/[Client]/` → `25_NURTURE/[Client]-Pending/`
- [ ] Keep all files intact (MAP findings still valid)

**Follow-Up Schedule:**

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Agreed date (30/60/90 days) | Check-in email |
| Reference original reason | Ask if circumstances changed |
| Offer to pick up where left off | No need to re-MAP |

**If Ready Later:** → Jump to CHART (abbreviated) → LAUNCH
**If Still Not Ready:** → Continue nurture
**If No Response:** → See PATH D

### PATH C: Walk Away

**When to Walk Away:**
- Won't commit time for MAP
- Wants price before understanding
- Wants guaranteed outcomes you can't control
- Argues about price before understanding scope
- "Tried everything" - learned helplessness
- Disrespectful of time or expertise
- Vibe is off / dread working with them

**Walk Away Script:**
> "I've really appreciated learning about your business. Based on what I'm hearing, I don't think I'm the right fit for what you need right now. [Brief reason if appropriate.] I'd rather be upfront about that than waste your time."

**Actions:**
- [ ] Update CRM: Mark deal "Direction Protocol - Walk Away"
- [ ] Note reason for walk away
- [ ] Archive folder: `90_ARCHIVE/[Year]/[Client]-WalkAway/`

**Remember:** A bad client costs more than no client.

### PATH D: Client Goes Dark

**Follow-Up Sequence:**

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 2 | "Just following up on our conversation..." |
| Day 7 | "Wanted to circle back - any questions I can answer?" |
| Day 14 | Phone call attempt + email |
| Day 30 | Final email: "Closing the loop on this" |

**After 30 Days of Silence:**
- [ ] Update CRM: Mark deal "Direction Protocol - Unresponsive"
- [ ] Archive folder: `90_ARCHIVE/[Year]/[Client]-NoResponse/`
- [ ] Retain files for 2 years (they may return)

## Pipeline Punks Alternative Path

For prospects who are qualified but can't afford full service:

### When to Offer Pipeline Punks

| Situation | Recommend |
|-----------|-----------|
| Budget <$1,500 + Has 10-20 hours | Pipeline Punks (DIY education) |
| Budget <$1,500 + No time | Revisit in 3-6 months |
| Budget $1,500-$3,000 + DIY mindset | Pipeline Punks |
| Budget $1,500-$3,000 + Needs it done | Command Center Build (entry) |
| Budget >$3,000 | Command Center Build (full) |

### The Credit Bridge

Script:
> "If you start with Pipeline Punks and later realize you don't have the time, we'll give you a 50% credit toward having us build it. So if you invest $500 in training, you'd get $250 off the Command Center Build."

### Pipeline Punks Follow-Up

- Track separately in CRM
- Check in at 30, 60, 90 days
- Look for signals they're stuck
- Offer to re-engage when ready

## ARO Assessment Integration

### When to Recommend ARO Assessment

During ASSESS or MAP, if the prospect:
- Has AI tools that aren't working consistently
- Wants automation but doesn't know where to start
- Has significant operational chaos that needs diagnosis

**Script:**
> "Based on what you're describing, I'd recommend we start with an ARO Assessment. It's a focused diagnostic that shows exactly where your operations are hitting friction and what needs to change before any tools will work well. Takes about a week, and you get a complete roadmap. If you proceed with implementation, we credit $500 toward the project."

### ARO Assessment Pricing

| Tier | Price | When |
|------|-------|------|
| Standard | $1,500 | Simple operation, single workflow |
| Professional | $2,000 | Multiple workflows, 4-10 people |
| Enterprise | $2,500 | Complex operation, 10+ people |

### ARO → Direction Protocol Flow

If client completes ARO and wants implementation:
1. ARO findings become MAP findings (no need to re-MAP)
2. Jump to CHART stage
3. Apply $500 credit
4. Proceed to LAUNCH
