## STAGE 0: Lead Generation

**Purpose:** Identify potential ARO Assessment candidates from various sources.

### Lead Sources

| Source | Description | Typical Quality |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Inbound | Website contact, content response | High |
| Referral | Existing client or partner referral | Highest |
| Networking | Event, meeting, introduction | Medium-High |
| Outbound | Cold outreach response | Medium |

### Initial Qualifiers

Before moving to IDENTIFY, check:

- [ ] Business has AI/automation frustration OR operational chaos
- [ ] Size appears to be 5-20 employees
- [ ] Owner or decision-maker is accessible
- [ ] Not obviously wrong fit (startup, enterprise, just wants an app)

### Lead Signals That Indicate ARO Assessment Fit

**Strong Signals (Proceed to IDENTIFY):**
- "Our AI tools don't work consistently"
- "We tried ChatGPT but it doesn't understand our business"
- "We want automation but don't know where to start"
- "Everything runs through me"
- "I can't take a day off"
- "We can't find anything when we need it"

**Weak Signals (May need different approach):**
- "We just need a specific app built" → Direct to Direction Protocol
- "We need enterprise software" → Not ICP
- "We're a startup figuring things out" → Too early

## STAGE 1: Direction Protocol - IDENTIFY

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Purpose:** Determine if there's enough fit to justify ARO Assessment recommendation
**Outcome:** Schedule Assessment OR polite exit

### The Three Core Questions

Ask these to understand their situation:

1. **"Tell me about your operation. What do you do, and how many people are involved?"**
   - Listen for: Size, complexity, owner involvement
   - Good: 5-20 people, owner drowning
   - Bad: Too small (<5) or too large (20+)

2. **"What's the thing that keeps you up at night about the business right now?"**
   - Listen for: Operational pain, visibility issues
   - Good: "I find out about problems too late"
   - Bad: "I need more marketing" (wrong problem)

3. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing tomorrow, what would it be?"**
   - Listen for: Specific operational issue
   - Good: "See what my team is doing in real-time"
   - Bad: "Double my revenue" (too vague)

### Pain Signals to Listen For

**Qualified Pain (Proceed):**
- [ ] "I can't see what's happening"
- [ ] "I find out about problems too late"
- [ ] "Everything depends on me"
- [ ] "I can't take a day off"
- [ ] "My guys don't do things consistently"
- [ ] "I'm drowning in spreadsheets / paper / texts"
- [ ] "Our AI tools give inconsistent results"

**Disqualifiers (Exit Gracefully):**
- [ ] "I just need someone to build me an app"
- [ ] "I want AI to handle everything"
- [ ] "What's the cheapest option?"
- [ ] "I don't really have a problem, just exploring"
- [ ] Won't commit to a longer conversation
- [ ] Argues about needing to understand before quoting

### Qualification Checklist

Before recommending ARO Assessment:

- [ ] Real operational problem (not just "want automation")
- [ ] Decision-maker on the call
- [ ] Sounds coachable (not blaming everyone else)
- [ ] Right size (5-20 people)
- [ ] Has AI/automation pain or aspiration
- [ ] Budget available ($1,500+ for assessment)

### IDENTIFY Outcomes

**IF QUALIFIED:**
1. Explain ARO Assessment briefly:
   > "Before we build anything, I'd recommend we assess your operations. This is a diagnostic that shows exactly where AI tools are hitting friction and what needs to change. Takes about a week, and you get a complete roadmap."

2. Mention pricing:
   > "Investment is $1,500-$2,500 depending on complexity. If you proceed with implementation, we credit $500 toward the project."

3. Next step:
   > "First step is an intake questionnaire - takes about 45 minutes to complete. Once I have that, we'll schedule a 90-minute discovery call."

4. CRM: Create deal "ARO Assessment - Lead"

**IF NOT QUALIFIED:**
Script:
> "I've really appreciated learning about your business. Based on what I'm hearing, I don't think I'm the right fit for what you need right now. [Optional: brief, honest reason.] I'd rather be upfront about that than waste your time."

## STAGE 3: Assessment Execution

**Duration:** Days 1-7 (varies by tier)
**Purpose:** Conduct discovery, analyze operations, generate deliverables
**Outcome:** 4 deliverables ready for handoff

### Day 1-2: Discovery

#### Pre-Call Preparation (30-60 min)

**Review intake questionnaire responses.**

**Claude Prompt:**
```
Here are [Client]'s intake questionnaire responses:
[Paste responses]

Prepare me for the discovery call - what should I dig into?
Identify red flags, opportunities, and specific follow-up questions based on their responses.
```

**Pre-call checklist:**
- [ ] Reviewed intake questionnaire thoroughly
- [ ] Identified 3-5 areas to dig into
- [ ] Prepared specific follow-up questions
- [ ] Have note-taking ready

#### Discovery Call (90 min)

**Opening (5 min):**
Script:
> "Thanks for completing the questionnaire. Today's goal is to really understand how information flows in your business so we can identify where AI tools are hitting friction. I'll ask a lot of questions - don't worry about perfect answers, I just want to understand how things actually work."

**Section 1: Current AI Usage (15 min)**
- What AI tools are you using right now?
- Where do they work well?
- Where do they frustrate you?
- What do you wish they could do that they can't?

*Listen for: "It doesn't understand our business" or "It gives wrong answers" = context architecture problems*

**Section 2: Information Architecture (20 min)**
- Where does your customer data live?
- Where does your financial data live?
- Where does project/job information live?
- If I needed to know [specific thing], where would I find it?
- What information exists only in someone's head?

*Listen for: Multiple systems, manual lookups, "it depends who you ask" = data structure problems*

**Section 3: Process Documentation (15 min)**
- Do you have written SOPs?
- How does a new person learn "how things work here"?
- What decisions require a specific person's judgment?
- Walk me through [most common workflow] step by step

*Listen for: "Ask Sarah" or "I just know" = process documentation gaps*

**Section 4: Decision Logic (15 min)**
- How do you prioritize when everything's urgent?
- What determines whether [common decision] goes path A or B?
- What's the criteria for [another common decision]?

*Listen for: Vague criteria, case-by-case thinking = decision logic gaps*

**Section 5: Technology Landscape (15 min)**
- What systems don't talk to each other that should?
- Where is data entered twice?
- What breaks when [key person] is unavailable?
- What integrations exist that you're not using?

*Listen for: Redundant data entry, person dependencies = access and integration gaps*

**Wrap-Up (5 min):**
Script:
> "This has been really helpful. I have a clear picture of where the friction points are. I'll spend the next few days analyzing everything and building your deliverables. You'll get them 24-48 hours before our handoff call so you can review. Any questions before we wrap?"

#### Post-Call (30 min)

- [ ] Save discovery notes immediately: `discovery-notes.md`
- [ ] Note top 3-5 friction points observed
- [ ] Note any surprises or red flags
- [ ] Update CRM with discovery notes summary

| Score | Meaning |

| 3 | Functional - Works but with friction |
| 4 | Good - Mostly organized, minor gaps |
| 5 | Agent-Ready - Explicit, consistent, accessible |

#### Gap Analysis

For each area scoring below 4:
- [ ] Document what friction this causes for AI agents
- [ ] Define what the improved state would look like
- [ ] Categorize difficulty: Quick win / Medium effort / Major project
- [ ] Estimate business impact of fixing

## STAGE 4: Handoff Call

**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Purpose:** Present findings, discuss options, get decision
**Outcome:** Client chooses PATH A (YES), B (DIY), C (Not Now), or D (Goes Dark)

### Pre-Handoff Checklist

**Deliverables Ready:**
- [ ] Context Architecture Report complete (10-15 pages)
- [ ] Implementation Roadmap complete (3-5 pages)
- [ ] Context Graph customized (8-10 pages)
- [ ] Documentation System Recommendation complete

**Logistics:**
- [ ] Deliverables sent to client 24-48 hrs before call
- [ ] "Deliverables Ready" email sent (EMAIL-TEMPLATES.md #4)
- [ ] Implementation proposal ready (if likely to proceed)
- [ ] Talking points prepared

### Handoff Call Agenda

#### Opening (5 min)

Script:
> "Thanks for taking the time to review the deliverables. Today I'll walk you through what we found, what it means for your operations, and what your options are going forward. Let's start with the big picture."

- Confirm they received and reviewed deliverables
- Ask if they have initial questions

#### Context Architecture Report Review (20 min)

**Overall Score:**
> "Your overall score is [X.X] out of 5. Here's what that means..."

**Top 3 Friction Points:**
For each:
1. What's happening now (specific example from discovery)
2. What it's costing them (time, money, stress, missed opportunities)
3. What "fixed" looks like

**ROI Opportunity:**
> "Based on what we found, addressing these friction points could [specific benefit]..."

#### Implementation Roadmap Review (15 min)

**Quick Wins (0-30 days):**
- Highlight 1-2 most impactful
- Emphasize achievability
- Show quick value

**Phase 1 (30-90 days):**
- Key changes and dependencies
- What improves after Phase 1

**Phase 2 (90-180 days):**
- Bigger structural changes
- Where this positions them long-term

#### Implementation Options Discussion (10 min)

**Present Three Paths:**

**1. DIY Path:**
> "You can implement this yourself using the roadmap. You'll have 30 days of email support from us, and all the deliverables are yours to keep. If you want hands-on guidance, we offer training workshops at $500-$1,500."

- Time investment: Estimate based on roadmap
- Support included: 30 days email
- Add-on option: ARO Training workshop

**2. TNDS-Led Path:**
> "We can implement this for you. Based on your assessment, I'd recommend [Command Center Build / Command Center + Battle Rhythm / Command Partner]. Timeline is [X weeks], investment is $[amount]. That includes the $500 credit from your assessment."

- What we do for them
- Timeline (8-12 weeks typical for Comprehensive)
- Investment range: $5,000-$12,000
- $500 assessment credit applied

**3. Hybrid Path:**
> "We can also split the work - you handle [X], we handle [Y]. This is good if budget is tight but you want expert help on the complex parts."

#### Questions (10 min)

- Open floor for questions
- Address concerns directly
- Clarify anything unclear

#### Decision/Next Steps (5 min)

**Ask for decision:**
> "Based on what we've discussed, which path makes the most sense for you?"

**If YES (TNDS Implementation):**
> "Great. I'll send over the implementation agreement today. Once signed, we'll get your kickoff scheduled within the week. The $500 credit is already applied."

**If DIY:**
> "Understood. You have everything you need in the deliverables. I'll send a follow-up email with the DIY resources and remind you about the 30-day support window. If you get stuck, we're here."

**If NOT NOW:**
> "When would be a good time to revisit this? I'll set a reminder and check in then. Everything we've done stays valid."

**If UNSURE:**
> "Would it help to have a couple days to think about it? I can follow up [specific day] to answer any other questions."

## Key Process Integrations

### ARO Assessment → Command Protocol

**When:** Client says YES to TNDS implementation

**Trigger:** Implementation agreement signed

**Handoff Checklist:**
- [ ] Context Graph v1.0 becomes v2.0 (living document)
- [ ] Assessment folder archived in 00-ARO-Assessment/
- [ ] Implementation folder created (01-Implementation/)
- [ ] Service determined (Command Center Build, Battle Rhythm, Command Partner)
- [ ] Implementation kickoff scheduled

**What Command Protocol Receives:**
- Complete Context Graph with client context
- Implementation Roadmap with prioritized work
- Context Architecture Report for reference
- All discovery notes and scoring

### ARO Assessment → Direction Protocol (Circular)

**When:** DIY clients get stuck and return for help

**Path:** Skip full assessment → Go to Direction Protocol CHART stage

**Why:** They already have:
- Context Graph (may need update)
- Implementation Roadmap (still valid)
- Understanding of gaps

**Action:**
1. Quick operational review (30 min) - what's changed since assessment?
2. Update Context Graph if needed
3. Create implementation proposal
4. Proceed to Command Protocol

### ARO Assessment Stand-Alone
