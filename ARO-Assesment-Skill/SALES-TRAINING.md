# Part 1: Discovery Question Guide

## Purpose of Discovery

The IDENTIFY call (Direction Protocol Stage 1) serves three purposes:
1. **Qualify** — Is this a fit for TNDS services?
2. **Understand** — What are their real pain points?
3. **Position** — Set up the ARO Assessment as the logical next step

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Goal:** End with assessment scheduled OR clear disqualification

---

## Question Framework: PAIN → IMPACT → CAPACITY → TIMELINE

### Section 1: Pain Discovery

*Objective: Understand what's broken and how bad it is*

**Opening Question:**
> "Tell me about your business. What does a typical day look like for you?"

*Listen for: Chaos signals, manual processes, frustrations*

**Follow-Up Pain Questions:**

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "What's the most frustrating part of running [their business type]?" | Primary pain point |
| "Walk me through how you handle [specific process they mentioned]." | Process maturity |
| "What falls through the cracks most often?" | System gaps |
| "How many hours a week do you spend on admin/ops vs. actual client work?" | Time allocation |
| "What would you do with 10 extra hours a week?" | Motivation/vision |

**Digging Deeper:**
> "You mentioned [pain point]. Can you give me a specific recent example?"

> "How long has this been a problem?"

> "What have you tried to fix it?"

---

### Section 2: Impact Quantification

*Objective: Put numbers to the pain*

**Impact Questions:**

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "When [pain point] happens, what does it cost you? Time? Money? Clients?" | Direct cost |
| "How many times per week/month does this happen?" | Frequency |
| "What's the ripple effect when this goes wrong?" | Secondary impact |
| "Have you lost any clients or opportunities because of this?" | Revenue impact |
| "If this were fixed, what would that be worth to you?" | Value perception |

**Quantification Prompts:**
> "If I had to guess, this is costing you [X hours/week or $X/month]. Does that sound right, or is it more/less?"

> "On a scale of 1-10, how painful is this problem? What would make it a 10?"

---

### Section 3: Capacity Assessment

*Objective: Understand their ability to change*

**Capacity Questions:**

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "Who handles your tech/systems currently?" | Technical capacity |
| "How much time could you dedicate to improving operations?" | Time availability |
| "Have you tried implementing new systems before? How did it go?" | Change history |
| "Who else would need to be involved in this decision?" | Decision process |
| "What's your team situation — just you, or do you have staff?" | Team size |

**Budget Signals (Indirect):**
> "Have you invested in operational improvements before? What did that look like?"

> "When you think about solving this, do you picture doing it yourself, hiring someone, or a hybrid?"

---

### Section 4: Timeline & Urgency

*Objective: Understand when they need to act*

**Timeline Questions:**

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "Is there a deadline driving this? A busy season, a launch, a hire?" | External pressure |
| "If you don't fix this in the next 3 months, what happens?" | Consequence of inaction |
| "What's your ideal timeline for having this sorted out?" | Expectation |
| "What would need to happen for you to move forward this month?" | Buying readiness |

**Urgency Gauging:**
> "On a priority list of 1-10, where does this fall right now?"

> "What else is competing for your attention/budget right now?"

---

## Qualifying Questions

**Must-Have Criteria:**

| Criterion | Question to Ask | Green Flag | Red Flag |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|----------|
| Real pain | "How urgent is this?" | Specific, quantified pain | Vague, theoretical |
| Budget | "Is solving this a line item?" | Has allocated budget | "I'll figure it out" |
| Authority | "Who makes the final call?" | They decide or influencer | Committee, unclear |
| Timeline | "When do you need this fixed?" | Next 90 days | "Someday" |
| Fit | "What tools do you use?" | Google Workspace, standard stack | Highly custom, enterprise |

**Disqualification Signals:**

- No specific pain (just "exploring")
- No budget ("I don't have money for this")
- Can't make decisions (need board approval, partner buy-in)
- Unrealistic expectations (want everything for nothing)
- Bad technology fit (SAP, custom legacy systems)
- Hostile to AI ("I don't trust automation")

# Part 2: Assessment Pitch Script

## The ARO Assessment Pitch

### Opening Frame (30 seconds)

> "Before we talk about solutions, we need to understand exactly what's going on in your operations. That's what the ARO Assessment does."

> "ARO stands for Agent-Ready Operations. It's a diagnostic that shows you exactly where your business is optimized for AI automation — and where the friction is."

### What It Is (60 seconds)

> "Here's how it works:"
>
> "**First**, you complete a short intake questionnaire — takes about 15 minutes. This gives me background on your tech stack and processes."
>
> "**Then**, we do a 90-minute discovery call. I dig deep into your six core operational areas: file organization, process documentation, data structure, decision logic, communication records, and access management."
>
> "**After that**, I score each area on a 1-5 scale and build out your deliverables. This takes about a week."
>
> "**Finally**, we do a 60-minute handoff call where I walk you through everything and we talk about next steps."

### What You Get (60 seconds)

> "You walk away with four things:"
>
> "**One:** A Context Architecture Report — this is your 10-15 page diagnostic. It shows your scores, explains why you got them, and prioritizes your friction points."
>
> "**Two:** An Implementation Roadmap — this breaks down what to fix into Quick Wins, Phase 1, and Phase 2. It includes timelines and budget estimates whether you DIY or hire us."
>
> "**Three:** A Context Graph — this is like an instruction manual for your business that an AI agent can use. It's a living document you'll use going forward."
>
> "**Four:** A Documentation Tier Recommendation — this tells you what level of documentation your business needs based on your complexity."

### The Value Proposition (45 seconds)

> "Most businesses waste thousands of dollars on automation that doesn't stick because they skip this diagnostic step. They build on a shaky foundation."
>
> "The assessment tells you exactly what to fix, in what order, and whether you need help doing it."
>
> "If you decide to work with us on implementation, the $500 assessment credit applies. If you decide to DIY, you have everything you need to do it right."
>
> "Either way, you're not guessing anymore."

### Investment & Tiers (30 seconds)

> "The assessment investment depends on your business complexity:"
>
> "**Standard** is $1,500 — that's for solo operators with a straightforward tech stack."
>
> "**Professional** is $2,000 — that's for small teams with multiple systems to evaluate."
>
> "**Enterprise** is $2,500 — that's for complex operations or compliance-heavy industries."
>
> "Based on what you've told me, you'd be at the **[Tier]** level."

### Close (15 seconds)

> "The next step is the intake questionnaire. I can send that over today, and once you complete it, we'll get your discovery call on the calendar."
>
> "Does that work for you?"

## Pitch Variations

### For the Skeptic

*When they need more proof:*

> "I get it — you want to know this isn't just another consulting exercise that sits on a shelf."
>
> "Here's what's different: Everything I deliver is designed to be used by AI agents, not just read by humans. The Context Graph, for example, becomes the foundation for any automation you build. It's not a report that collects dust — it's a working document."
>
> "And the roadmap isn't vague. It tells you exactly what to do, in what order, with real timelines and budgets."

### For the DIYer

*When they want to do it themselves:*

> "That's actually the point. The assessment gives you everything you need to implement this yourself if you want to."
>
> "What I hear from DIY clients is that they can usually handle 60-70% of the recommendations themselves. The assessment shows you which 60-70% that is — and which parts are worth getting help on."
>
> "Plus, you get 30 days of email support either way, so if you hit a wall, I'm there."

### For the Budget-Conscious

*When they're worried about cost:*

> "I hear you on budget. Here's how I think about it:"
>
> "You mentioned [their pain point] is costing you roughly [X hours/week or $X/month]. If that's accurate, the assessment pays for itself in [timeframe] just by showing you how to fix that one thing."
>
> "And if you do move to implementation, the $500 credit applies. So you're really investing $[assessment - 500] in the diagnostic if you continue."

### For the Urgent Buyer

*When they need results fast:*

> "I hear the urgency. Here's what I'd recommend:"
>
> "The assessment identifies Quick Wins — things you can fix in the next 30 days with minimal effort. We can start on those immediately."
>
> "Some clients start implementing Quick Wins while we're still building the full deliverables. By the time we do the handoff call, you've already made progress."

# Part 3: Objection Handling Guide

## The Objection Handling Framework

**LAER Method:**
1. **Listen** — Let them finish. Don't interrupt.
2. **Acknowledge** — Show you heard them. "I understand..."
3. **Explore** — Ask a question to understand the real concern.
4. **Respond** — Address the specific concern, not a generic rebuttal.

---

## Common Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "That's more than I expected"

**What they're really saying:** I need to justify this expense.

**Response:**
> "I hear you. Let me ask — when you said [pain point] was costing you [X hours/X dollars], was that accurate?"
>
> *[Wait for confirmation]*
>
> "So if the assessment helps you fix even half of that, it pays for itself in [timeframe]. Does that math work for you?"

**If they push back:**
> "What number would feel more comfortable? I want to understand where the gap is."
>
> *[If they're far off, they may not be qualified]*

---

### Objection 2: "I can figure this out myself"

**What they're really saying:** I don't see the value of paying for something I could do.

**Response:**
> "You absolutely could. The question is whether it's the best use of your time."
>
> "How many hours would it take you to do a comprehensive operational audit, score yourself objectively, and build out a prioritized roadmap?"
>
> *[Let them answer]*
>
> "At your hourly rate, is that more or less than the assessment investment?"

**Alternative Response:**
> "Most DIY clients tell me they can handle 60-70% of the work themselves. The assessment shows you exactly which 60-70% to focus on — and which 30-40% would be a struggle."
>
> "Think of it like a doctor's diagnostic before you start treatment. You wouldn't skip the diagnosis and just start taking random medicine."

---

### Objection 3: "I don't have time for this right now"

**What they're really saying:** This isn't a priority OR I'm overwhelmed.

**Response:**
> "I get it — you're slammed. That's actually why the assessment exists."
>
> "The intake questionnaire is 15 minutes. The discovery call is 90 minutes. The handoff is 60 minutes. That's less than 3 hours of your time total."
>
> "In exchange, you get a clear roadmap so you're not constantly putting out fires. Would investing 3 hours now save you more than 3 hours in the next month?"

**If they're truly overwhelmed:**
> "Sounds like you're in firefighting mode. When do you think you'll have bandwidth to work on this — 30 days? 60 days?"
>
> "Let's put a reminder on the calendar for [date]. I'll check back then, and if the timing still isn't right, no pressure."

---

### Objection 4: "I've been burned by consultants before"

**What they're really saying:** I don't trust this will deliver value.

**Response:**
> "I've heard that a lot, honestly. What happened with the last one?"
>
> *[Listen to their story]*
>
> "That sounds frustrating. Here's how this is different:"
>
> "First, everything I deliver is designed to be actionable, not just 'insights.' The roadmap tells you exactly what to do, not vague recommendations."
>
> "Second, the deliverables are designed for AI agents to use, not just humans to read. The Context Graph becomes a working document for automation."
>
> "Third, if you're not satisfied with the deliverables, I'll keep working until they're useful. I'm not in the business of delivering shelf-ware."

---

### Objection 5: "Can't you just tell me what to do?"

**What they're really saying:** I want the answer without the diagnostic.

**Response:**
> "I could give you generic advice right now, but it probably wouldn't stick."
>
> "Here's why: Every business has different friction points. What works for one client might be completely wrong for you. The assessment tells us exactly where YOUR bottlenecks are."
>
> "It's like going to the doctor and saying 'just give me something for the pain.' They need to diagnose first to treat effectively."

---

### Objection 6: "What if I don't like what you find?"

**What they're really saying:** I'm afraid of bad news.

**Response:**
> "That's actually a good thing."
>
> "If everything were perfect, you wouldn't be talking to me. The assessment isn't about judging you — it's about finding the quick wins."
>
> "Most clients score between 2 and 4. That's normal. A low score just means there's a lot of opportunity to improve."
>
> "Would you rather not know where the problems are?"

---

### Objection 7: "I need to talk to my partner/spouse/team"

**What they're really saying:** Either they're not the decision-maker, or they need validation.

**Response:**
> "Totally fair. What questions do you think they'll have?"
>
> *[If they list questions, answer them now]*
>
> "Would it be helpful if I sent you a summary you could share with them?"
>
> "When are you meeting with them? Let's put a follow-up on the calendar for after that."

**If they're stalling:**
> "Is this a 'yes' from you pending their approval, or are you still deciding yourself?"
>
> *[This clarifies where the real objection is]*

---

### Objection 8: "What's included in the implementation?"

**What they're really saying:** I'm thinking ahead to the next step.

**Response:**
> "Great question — let's talk about that after the assessment, because it depends on what we find."
>
> "The assessment will show us exactly what needs to be built. From there, we'll recommend one of three services: a one-time Command Center Build, an ongoing Battle Rhythm partnership, or a full Command Partner engagement."
>
> "The investment for implementation typically ranges from $5,000 for a simple build to $5,000/month for a full partnership. Your $500 assessment credit applies to any of those."
>
> "But first, let's diagnose before we prescribe. Does that make sense?"

---

### Objection 9: "I'm already using [competitor/alternative]"

**What they're really saying:** I've already solved this OR why should I switch?

**Response:**
> "Good — how's that working for you?"
>
> *[Listen for gaps or frustrations]*
>
> "What made you take this call if you've already got something in place?"
>
> *[Their answer reveals the real need]*
>
> "The assessment isn't about replacing what you have — it's about making sure everything works together. Often we find gaps between tools, not gaps in the tools themselves."

---

### Objection 10: "I don't trust AI / I'm skeptical about automation"

**What they're really saying:** I have concerns about technology.

**Response:**
> "That's a healthy skepticism. Can I ask what specifically concerns you?"
>
> *[Listen to their concern]*
>
> "Here's my philosophy: AI should eliminate the work you hate, not replace the work you love."
>
> "The assessment isn't about automating everything. It's about finding the repetitive, time-consuming stuff that's draining you — and making that disappear."
>
> "You stay in control of the decisions and the client relationships. The automation handles the admin."

---

## Closing After Objections

Once you've handled the objection:

> "Does that address your concern?"
>
> *[Wait for confirmation]*
>
> "Great. So the next step is the intake questionnaire. I can send that over right now — does that work?"

If they raise another objection, handle it. If they keep raising objections, they may not be qualified:

> "It sounds like the timing might not be right. Should we table this and reconnect in [30/60/90 days]?"

---

## Red Flags: When to Walk Away

| Signal | What It Means | What to Say |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| They want a guarantee of specific ROI | Unrealistic expectations | "I can't guarantee outcomes I haven't diagnosed yet. That's why we do the assessment first." |
| They want to negotiate before seeing value | Price-focused, not value-focused | "Let's make sure this is the right fit before we talk numbers." |
| They're comparing to a $99 online course | Wrong value frame | "This isn't a course — it's a custom diagnostic for your business." |
| They're hostile or rude | Bad client relationship | "I want to make sure this is a good fit for both of us. Let me think about whether we're the right partner." |
| They can't articulate their problem | No real pain | "It sounds like you're still figuring out what you need. Let's reconnect when you have more clarity." |
