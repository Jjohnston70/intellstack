# ARO Assessment Handoff Protocol

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 28, 2026
**Purpose:** Detailed guide for executing handoff calls and managing post-handoff actions

---

## Overview

The handoff call is where the assessment converts to implementation (or doesn't). This document provides:

1. **Pre-Handoff Checklist** — Everything ready before scheduling
2. **Handoff Call Agenda** — Minute-by-minute structure
3. **Post-Handoff Actions** — Checklists for each outcome path

**Goal:** Client leaves the call knowing exactly what was found, what it means, and what their options are.

---

## PART 2: Handoff Call Agenda

**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Format:** Video call (screen share ready)
**Attendees:** Client decision-maker(s), you

### Pre-Call Setup (5 min before)

- [ ] Test video/audio
- [ ] Have deliverables open and ready to screen share
- [ ] Have discovery notes accessible
- [ ] Have implementation proposal ready (don't share yet)
- [ ] Mute notifications

---

### Opening (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Set the stage, confirm they reviewed materials

**Script:**
> "Thanks for making time for this. Before we dive in, I want to make sure you had a chance to look through the deliverables I sent. Did you get a chance to review them?"

**If YES:**
> "Great. Any initial questions or things that jumped out at you?"

**If NO:**
> "No problem — I'll walk you through everything. By the end of this call, you'll have a clear picture of where things stand and what your options are."

**Transition:**
> "Today I'll walk you through what we found, what it means for your operations, and what your options are going forward. Feel free to stop me with questions at any point."

---

### Context Architecture Report Review (20 minutes)

**Goal:** Client understands their current state and the gaps

#### Overall Score (3 min)

**Screen share:** Executive Summary page

**Script:**
> "Your overall ARO score is [X.X] out of 5. To put that in context: a 5 means your operations are fully agent-ready — AI tools would work seamlessly. A 1 means total chaos. You're at [X.X], which means [interpretation]."

**Interpretation guide:**
- 4.0-5.0: "You're in good shape. A few tweaks and you're there."
- 3.0-3.9: "Solid foundation, but some friction points are limiting what AI can do for you."
- 2.0-2.9: "There's real friction here. AI tools are fighting your operations, not helping them."
- 1.0-1.9: "Significant structural issues. This is why things feel chaotic."

#### Top 3 Friction Points (15 min)

**For each friction point (5 min each):**

**1. State the problem:**
> "The first thing we found is [friction point]. In your case, this showed up when [specific example from discovery]."

**2. Explain the impact:**
> "What this costs you is [time/money/stress/missed opportunities]. You mentioned during our discovery call that [quote or paraphrase their pain]."

**3. Paint "fixed":**
> "If we fix this, [specific outcome]. Instead of [current pain], you'd have [improved state]."

**Questions to ask after each:**
> "Does that match what you're experiencing?"
> "Anything I'm missing there?"

#### Quick Score Breakdown (2 min)

**Screen share:** Scoring section

> "Here's how you scored across all six areas. [Walk through briefly — don't belabor each one]. The ones that need the most attention are [X, Y, Z]."

---

### Implementation Roadmap Review (15 minutes)

**Goal:** Client sees a clear path forward with options

#### Quick Wins Preview (5 min)

**Screen share:** Quick Wins section

**Script:**
> "These are things you can do in the next 30 days that will make an immediate difference. They're designed to be low-effort, high-impact."

**Highlight 2-3:**
> "For example, [Quick Win 1] — this would [specific outcome]. And [Quick Win 2] — this addresses [specific friction point]."

#### Phase 1 Overview (4 min)

**Screen share:** Phase 1 section

**Script:**
> "Phase 1 is the 30-90 day window. This is where we tackle [main theme]. The big changes here are [list 2-3 key items]."

> "After Phase 1, you'd have [specific capability you don't have now]."

#### Phase 2 Overview (3 min)

**Screen share:** Phase 2 section

**Script:**
> "Phase 2 is the 90-180 day window. This is the bigger structural work — [brief overview]. This is where you'd see [longer-term outcome]."

#### Investment Overview (3 min)

**Don't screen share pricing yet — just discuss verbally**

**Script:**
> "The roadmap has estimates for both doing this yourself and having us do it. For DIY, you're looking at [X hours] of effort over [Y months]. For TNDS-led implementation, we're in the [$X,XXX - $X,XXX] range depending on scope."

---

### Implementation Options Discussion (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Client understands their choices and feels informed, not pressured

#### Present Three Paths

**Path 1: DIY**

> "You can absolutely implement this yourself. You have the roadmap, you have the Context Graph, you have 30 days of email support from us. Many clients go this route, especially if they have the time and technical comfort."

> "The investment is your time — probably [X hours] over the next [X months] based on what we found."

> "If you want hands-on guidance, we offer a training workshop at $500-$1,500 where I walk you through implementation step by step."

**Path 2: TNDS-Led**

> "We can implement this for you. Based on your assessment, I'd recommend [Command Center Build / Command Center + Battle Rhythm / Command Partner]."

> "Timeline would be [X weeks]. Investment is $[amount]."

> "That includes the $500 credit from your assessment — so it's really $[amount minus 500]."

> "What you get is [brief value statement — visibility, accountability, systems that work without you]."

**Path 3: Hybrid**

> "We can also split the work. You handle the Quick Wins and Phase 1 documentation, we handle the technical build. This is good if you want to manage budget but still want expert help on the complex parts."

#### Gauge Interest

> "Based on what we've discussed, what's your initial reaction? Which path makes the most sense for where you are right now?"

**Listen carefully. Don't push.**

---

### Questions & Objections (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Address concerns honestly, without being defensive

#### Common Objections and Responses

**"That's expensive."**
> "I understand. Let me put it in context: you told me [pain point] is costing you [X hours/week or $X/month]. Over [timeframe], that's [larger number]. The investment to fix it is [amount]. The math usually works out."

> "Also, this is fixed scope, fixed price. No surprise invoices. No open-ended project."

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Of course. What questions would help you decide? What's missing that would make this clearer?"

> "Would it help if I followed up in a few days once you've had time to digest?"

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> "The pricing is based on the scope we identified. I can adjust scope if budget is a constraint — maybe we start with Phase 1 only, or focus on the top 2 friction points instead of all three. What's your budget range?"

**"We tried something like this before and it didn't work."**
> "Tell me more about that. What happened?"
> [Listen, then address the specific failure mode]
> "Here's how our approach is different: [specific difference]."

**"I'm not sure we have capacity for this right now."**
> "That's fair. Would it help to start smaller? The Quick Wins are designed to fit into your existing workflow. Or we could push start to [specific date] when things calm down."

#### Questions to Ask Them

> "What's your main concern right now?"
> "Is there anything in the assessment that surprised you?"
> "What would need to be true for you to move forward?"

---

### Decision & Next Steps (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Get a clear answer or a clear timeline for an answer

#### Ask for the Decision

**Script:**
> "So — based on everything we've discussed, what makes sense for you?"

**Wait for answer. Don't fill the silence.**

---

#### If YES (TNDS Implementation)

**Script:**
> "Great. Here's what happens next: I'll send you the implementation agreement today. Once you sign, we'll get your kickoff call scheduled — usually within a week. The $500 credit from your assessment is already applied."

**Immediate actions:**
- [ ] Confirm they're ready to receive proposal
- [ ] Note any scope adjustments discussed
- [ ] Confirm decision-maker will sign
- [ ] Ask about preferred kickoff timing

---

#### If DIY

**Script:**
> "Understood. You have everything you need in the deliverables. I'll send a follow-up email with the DIY resources and a reminder about the 30-day support window. If you get stuck on anything, email me — I'll respond within 24 hours."

> "And if you change your mind and want us to take over, the $500 credit still applies. Just let me know."

**Immediate actions:**
- [ ] Confirm they have all deliverables
- [ ] Remind about 30-day support
- [ ] Offer training workshop option
- [ ] Set Day 15 and Day 30 check-in reminders

---

#### If NOT NOW

**Script:**
> "I totally understand. Before we wrap up — when would be a good time to revisit this? I want to make sure I follow up at the right moment, not pester you."

> "Is there anything specific that would need to change for you to be ready?"

**Immediate actions:**
- [ ] Note their preferred follow-up timeline
- [ ] Note reason for delay
- [ ] Confirm deliverables are still valid when ready
- [ ] Remind $500 credit doesn't expire

---

#### If UNSURE

**Script:**
> "Would it help to have a couple days to think it over? I can follow up [specific day] to answer any other questions."

> "Is there anything specific that would help you decide?"

**Immediate actions:**
- [ ] Schedule specific follow-up (don't leave open-ended)
- [ ] Ask what information would help
- [ ] Offer to send additional details if helpful

---

### Closing (2 minutes)

**Script:**
> "Thanks for going through this with me. You've got a clear picture of where things stand and what your options are. [Summarize their decision or next step]."

> "Any final questions before we wrap up?"

**End call.**

## PART 3: Post-Handoff Action Checklists

Complete the appropriate checklist based on client decision.

---

### PATH A: Client Says YES

**Timeline:** Complete all items within 7 days

#### Immediate (Same Day)

**Email:**
- [ ] Send "Post-Handoff YES" email (EMAIL-TEMPLATES #5)
- [ ] Attach implementation proposal/agreement
- [ ] Confirm investment amount and timeline
- [ ] Mention $500 credit applied

**CRM:**
- [ ] Update deal stage to "Implementation - Proposal Sent"
- [ ] Update deal value to implementation amount
- [ ] Set expected close date (7-14 days)
- [ ] Log handoff call notes

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Create task: "Follow up on agreement" (Day 3 if not signed)
- [ ] Create task: "Schedule kickoff" (after signing)

#### Within 7 Days (After Agreement Signed)

**CRM:**
- [ ] Update deal to "Implementation - Closed Won"
- [ ] Record signed date
- [ ] Record first payment received

**File Management:**
- [ ] Create folder: `30_ACTIVE_CLIENTS/[Client-Name]/`
- [ ] Create subfolder: `30_ACTIVE_CLIENTS/[Client-Name]/00-ARO-Assessment/`

- [ ] Copy `context-graph.md` from `00-ARO-Assessment/` to `01-Implementation/`

**Implementation Prep:**
- [ ] Schedule kickoff call (within 7 days of signing)
- [ ] Create project structure based on Documentation Tier
- [ ] Prepare kickoff agenda

#### Immediate (Same Day)

- [ ] Include reminder about 30-day support

- [ ] Remind $500 credit available if they change mind

**CRM:**

- [ ] Mark as Closed-Won (assessment revenue)
- [ ] Create new deal: "Future Implementation - [Client] - Nurture"
- [ ] Set nurture deal value to estimated implementation ($5K-$12K)
- [ ] Log handoff call notes

**File Management:**
- [ ] Move folder: `20_PROSPECTS/[Client-Name]-ARO-Assessment/` → `40_PAST_CLIENTS/[Client-Name]-ARO-DIY/`
- [ ] Keep all files intact

**Support Period Setup:**
- [ ] Note: 30-day support period starts today
- [ ] Create task: "Day 15 DIY check-in"
- [ ] Create task: "Day 30 DIY check-in" (EMAIL-TEMPLATES #8)

#### Day 15

- [ ] Send brief check-in email
- [ ] Ask: How's implementation going? Any roadblocks?
- [ ] Assess: Still DIY or want help?

#### Day 30

- [ ] Send "30-Day DIY Check-In" email (EMAIL-TEMPLATES #8)
- [ ] Remind: Support period ending
- [ ] Offer: Training workshop or implementation if stuck
- [ ] Assess: Convert to implementation or continue nurture?

**If Converting to Implementation:**
- [ ] Move nurture deal to "Implementation - Proposal Sent"
- [ ] Send implementation proposal
- [ ] Apply $500 credit
- [ ] Follow PATH A from "Within 7 Days"

#### Ongoing (Day 60, 90+)

- [ ] Monthly check-in emails (brief, not pushy)
- [ ] Watch for return signals
- [ ] Update nurture deal as needed

---

### PATH C: Client Says "Not Now"

**Timeline:** Set up nurture sequence immediately

#### Immediate (Same Day)

**During Call:**
- [ ] Ask: "When should we revisit this?"
- [ ] Ask: "What would need to change for you to be ready?"
- [ ] Note their answers

**Email:**
- [ ] Send "Post-Handoff Not Now" email (EMAIL-TEMPLATES #7)
- [ ] Reference their stated timeline
- [ ] Remind: Deliverables valid indefinitely
- [ ] Remind: $500 credit honored when ready

**CRM:**
- [ ] Update deal to "ARO Assessment - Nurture"
- [ ] Set follow-up date per their preference
- [ ] Log reason for delay
- [ ] Log handoff call notes

**File Management:**
- [ ] Move folder: `20_PROSPECTS/[Client-Name]-ARO-Assessment/` → `25_NURTURE/[Client-Name]-ARO-Pending/`
- [ ] Keep all files intact

**Follow-Up Setup:**
- [ ] Create task: Follow-up at agreed date
- [ ] Create backup task: +30 days if no response to first follow-up

#### At Agreed Follow-Up Date

- [ ] Send "Nurture Follow-Up" email (EMAIL-TEMPLATES #9)
- [ ] Reference their original reason for delay
- [ ] Ask if circumstances have changed
- [ ] Offer to schedule call if interested

**If Ready to Proceed:**
- [ ] Move deal to "Implementation - Proposal Sent"
- [ ] Send implementation proposal
- [ ] Follow PATH A from "Within 7 Days"

**If Still Not Ready:**
- [ ] Log response
- [ ] Set next follow-up (+30 days)
- [ ] Continue nurture sequence

**If No Response:**
- [ ] Follow up again at +30 days
- [ ] If still no response after 90 days total → PATH D

---

### PATH D: Client Goes Dark

**Timeline:** Follow-up sequence over 30 days, then archive

#### Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 3:**
- [ ] Send follow-up email #1 (see EMAIL-TEMPLATES - Go Dark section)
- [ ] Subject: "RE: Your ARO Assessment Results"
- [ ] Tone: Friendly check-in

**Day 7:**
- [ ] Send follow-up email #2
- [ ] Different angle: "Any thoughts on which path?"
- [ ] Offer to schedule call

**Day 14:**
- [ ] Attempt phone call
- [ ] Leave voicemail if no answer
- [ ] Send email: "Tried to reach you by phone"

**Day 30:**
- [ ] Send final follow-up email
- [ ] Tone: Closing the loop
- [ ] Leave door open for future

#### After 30 Days of Silence

**CRM:**
- [ ] Update deal to "ARO Assessment - Unresponsive"
- [ ] Log all follow-up attempts
- [ ] Mark as Closed - No Response

**File Management:**
- [ ] Create folder (if needed): `90_ARCHIVE/[Year]/`
- [ ] Move folder: `20_PROSPECTS/[Client-Name]-ARO-Assessment/` → `90_ARCHIVE/[Year]/[Client-Name]-ARO-NoResponse/`
- [ ] Set reminder: Compress folder after 90 days

**Optional:**
- [ ] Set reminder: 6-month re-engagement attempt
- [ ] Retain files for 2 years
