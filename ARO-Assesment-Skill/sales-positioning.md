# ARO Sales Positioning

Talking points, objection handling, and framing for ARO assessment sales conversations.

---

## Core Value Proposition

> "You've probably noticed that AI tools are inconsistent — sometimes they nail it, sometimes they're useless. The difference usually isn't the AI. It's whether your business information is organized in a way the AI can understand."

> "We do an assessment that shows you exactly where your operations are creating friction for AI tools, and what it would take to fix it. You get a clear roadmap — whether you implement it yourself or have us do it."

> "Most businesses are organized for humans who can ask follow-up questions and fill in gaps. AI can't do that. We help you restructure so every AI tool you use — now and in the future — performs at its ceiling instead of its floor."

---

## Discovery Call Framework

### Opening (5 min)
- Thank them for their time
- Confirm what prompted the conversation
- Set agenda: understand their situation, share how we might help, answer questions

### Current State (20 min)
- What AI tools are you currently using?
- What are you using them for?
- What's working? What's frustrating?
- Walk me through a recent time it worked great
- Walk me through a recent time it failed

### Dig Into Failures (15 min)
- When it failed, what was happening?
- Did you have to re-explain context?
- Did it give you wrong information?
- Did it miss something obvious to you?

(Listen for: context gaps, inconsistent data, undocumented processes, tribal knowledge)

### Business Impact (10 min)
- How much time do you spend working around AI limitations?
- What would change if it worked consistently?
- What would you do with AI if it was reliable?

### Present Assessment Option (10 min)
- Here's what we do: [brief explanation of ARO Assessment]
- Takes 5-7 days
- You get [deliverables]
- Investment is [price range]

### Next Steps (5 min)
- Questions?
- If interested: "I'll send you the intake questionnaire today. Once you complete it, we'll schedule the discovery call."

**Timeline qualifier:**

## Objection Handling

### "We just need the automation, not an assessment."

> "I hear that. Here's what usually happens when we skip assessment: we build something that works in testing but fails in production because we didn't understand where your data actually lives or how decisions really get made. The assessment makes the implementation faster and more reliable. Think of it like a foundation inspection before a renovation."

**If they push back:**
> "Tell you what — if you proceed with implementation after the assessment, we'll credit $500 of the assessment toward the project. So you're not paying twice for discovery work."

### "Can you just give us a quote for the automation?"

> "I can give you a range, but without understanding your current setup, I'd be guessing. The assessment takes 5-7 days and gives both of us the information we need to scope accurately."

**Follow up:**
> "What's happened in the past when you've gotten automation work done — did they do any discovery first, or did they just jump in?"

(Often reveals past bad experiences that validate the assessment approach)

### "Our operations aren't that complicated."

> "That's actually ideal — simpler operations are faster to optimize. The assessment might be a quick win: we find 2-3 things to fix, you implement them, and you're done. Worst case, we confirm you're already well-organized and give you a template to maintain it."

### "We don't have time for this."

> "I get it — you're busy. The assessment requires about 2 hours of your time total: 45 minutes for the questionnaire, 90 minutes for the discovery call. The rest is our work. The ROI comes from every AI interaction working better from that point forward."

**If still hesitant:**
> "How much time do you currently spend re-explaining context to AI tools, cleaning up their output, or doing things manually because the AI doesn't work reliably?"

### "That's expensive for an assessment."

> "I understand. Here's what you're getting: not just a report, but a customized roadmap and template you can use forever. Most clients find one quick win from the assessment saves more than the cost in the first month."

**Alternative framing:**
> "Compare it to what you're losing: if AI inconsistency costs you 5 hours a week, that's 20 hours a month. At your billing rate, how long before the assessment pays for itself?"

### "Can't we just use ChatGPT/Claude better?"

> "You absolutely can get better at prompting — that's part of what we teach. But there's a ceiling to what prompting can fix. If your data is scattered or your processes aren't documented, no prompt in the world will give the AI what it needs. We help you raise the floor so that basic prompts work great, and advanced prompts work amazingly."

### "We tried something like this before and it didn't help."

> "What happened? I'd like to understand what didn't work."

(Listen carefully — usually it was:)
- Generic recommendations that didn't fit their business
- No implementation support
- Consultant didn't understand AI specifically
- Recommendations too complex to implement

> "That's exactly why we focus on AI-specific legibility, not general organization consulting. And our roadmap includes quick wins you can implement immediately, not just long-term transformation projects."

## Pricing Conversations

### Standard ($1,500)
> "For a solo operator or small team with one primary workflow to assess, the Standard assessment at $1,500 is the right fit. You get the full report, roadmap, and customized template."

### Professional ($2,000)
> "With multiple workflows and a team of 4-10 people, the Professional assessment at $2,000 makes more sense. We include additional stakeholder interviews to make sure we understand how different roles interact with information."

### Enterprise ($2,500)
> "For organizations over 10 people or with multiple locations, the Enterprise assessment at $2,500 includes department-specific recommendations and change management considerations."

### Add-Ons
> "Two optional add-ons: For $500, we'll create your first three Context Graphs based on findings — gives you working examples to follow. For $300, we do a live agent testing session showing before/after performance on one quick win."

## Urgency Creation

**Natural urgency:**
> "Every day you wait, you're accumulating AI interaction time that could be more effective. The assessment is 5-7 days — you could have your roadmap before the end of the month."

**Capacity framing:**
> "I typically take 2-3 assessments per month to ensure quality. I have availability in [timeframe]. After that, I'm booked into [next available]."

**Momentum framing:**
> "You're already thinking about this, you've already spent time on this call. The intake questionnaire takes 45 minutes — want me to send it over so you can capture your thoughts while they're fresh?"

## Follow-Up Sequences

### After Discovery Call (No Decision)

**Day 1:** Send summary email with next steps and questionnaire link

**Day 3:** "Following up on our conversation. Any questions I can answer?"

**Day 7:** "Still thinking about the ARO assessment? Happy to jump on a quick call to address any concerns."

**Day 14:** "Wanted to circle back one more time. If now isn't the right time, no problem — but I wanted to make sure you had everything you needed to decide."

### After Questionnaire Received

**Same day:** "Got your questionnaire — thank you! I'll review and we'll be ready for our discovery call on [date]. Here's the calendar link to confirm."

### After Assessment Delivered

**Day 1:** Handoff call

**Day 7:** "How's the implementation going? Any questions as you work through the quick wins?"

**Day 14:** "Wanted to check in. Have you had a chance to try any of the recommendations? Happy to help if you're running into obstacles."

**Day 30:** "Your 30-day support window is closing. Any final questions? Also wanted to mention we have availability for implementation work if you'd like help executing the roadmap."

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. General Business Consultants
> "Most business consultants organize for humans. We organize for AI agents. That's a specific technical discipline, not just 'getting organized.'"

### vs. AI Tool Vendors
> "Tool vendors want to sell you their tool. We're tool-agnostic — we optimize your operations so ANY AI tool performs better, now and in the future."

### vs. IT Consultants
> "IT consultants focus on systems and security. We focus on information architecture and process documentation — the stuff that determines whether AI tools actually work once they're connected."

### vs. DIY
> "You could absolutely do this yourself. We offer training for exactly that reason. But most business owners don't have time to become experts in information architecture. The question is whether your time is better spent running your business or restructuring your documentation."
