## Template 1: Mapping Session Proposal

**When to Use:** During Direction Protocol to position the value of a deep-dive mapping session
**Purpose:** Explain what mapping does and why it matters

---

**Subject:** Understanding Your Operation — Next Steps

---

Hi [First Name],

Based on our conversation about [main pain point], I'd like to propose a deeper dive called a **Process Mapping Session**.

**What It Is:**

A 90-minute working session where I walk through your [process/system] using a diagnostic framework that reveals:

- What information you're tracking vs. what's missing
- Where decisions are delayed or based on incomplete data
- Where your systems make predictions that aren't validated
- What "physics" of your business isn't built into your tools

**Why It Matters:**

Most business systems track **shadows** — what people report happened — not **reality** — what actually happened. The gap between the two is usually where the problems live.

This session produces a prioritized list of gaps and a specific next step, which gives you (and me) clarity on what actually needs to change.

**What You'll Get:**

1. Gap Report — Prioritized list of what's missing, ranked by impact
2. State Architecture — Map of how information flows (or doesn't)
3. Recommended Next Step — One concrete action with clear justification

**Investment:**

- 90 minutes of your time
- Have access to your main systems (spreadsheets, apps, etc.)
- Think of a recent example of something going wrong

**Next Step:**

If this sounds valuable, let's get it on the calendar. I have availability [Day 1] and [Day 2]. Which works better?

[Signature]

## Template 2: Pre-Session Preparation

**When to Use:** 48 hours before scheduled mapping session
**Purpose:** Help client prepare for maximum session value

---

**Subject:** Preparing for Our Mapping Session — [Day, Month DD]

---

Hi [First Name],

We're scheduled for our mapping session on **[Day, Month DD] at [Time]**.

**The Goal:**

By the end of our 90 minutes, you'll have a prioritized list of gaps in your [process] and a specific recommended first step.

**Please Prepare:**

**Before the Session:**

1. **Have access to your main systems** — Whatever you use to run [process]: apps, spreadsheets, dashboards
2. **Think of a recent failure or frustration** — A specific job, deal, or project that didn't go as planned
3. **Block focused time** — This works best without interruptions

**Questions to Consider:**

Take 10 minutes to think about (no need to write anything):

- What information do you wish you had at your fingertips right now?
- What's the last thing that surprised you about your operation?
- If a new employee asked "how do we know if things are on track?" — what would you say?

**Optional but Helpful:**

- Bring someone who knows "how things actually work" (operations person, senior tech, office manager)
- Have a sample report or dashboard you currently rely on

**What We'll Cover:**

I'll ask detailed questions across five areas:
1. What you're tracking and where it lives
2. What decisions those inform and how fast
3. What you're predicting (even implicitly)
4. How you know if predictions were right
5. What constraints can't be violated

**Session Details:**

**When:** [Day, Month DD, YYYY] at [X:XX am/pm MT]
**Duration:** 90 minutes (may run to 2 hours if we go deep)
**Format:** [Video call — link / In person — location]

Questions before then? Just reply here.

### Tone Guidelines

All templates follow TNDS voice:
- **Direct** — Get to the point, no fluff
- **Practical** — Focus on outcomes, not philosophy
- **No jargon** — Translate framework terms for client understanding
- **Outcome-focused** — Always tie back to what they get

### Framework Language Translation

When writing to clients, translate internal framework terms:

| Internal Term | Client-Friendly Version |
|--------------|------------------------|
| State space | What you track / what information you have |
| Shadow data | What someone reported vs. what actually happened |
| Feedback loop | How you know if predictions were right |
| Transition model | What you're predicting will happen next |
| Domain physics | Constraints that can't be changed |
| Brittleness | What breaks when things don't go as planned |
