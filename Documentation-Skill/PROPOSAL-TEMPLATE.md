# Proposal Template Guide

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 29, 2026
**Purpose:** Detailed guide for creating client proposals

---

## Overview

Proposals are created after MAP sessions to present findings and recommendations to clients. This guide provides the complete structure, section-by-section guidance, and variations for different service types.

---

## When to Create a Proposal

- MAP session complete
- Client is a qualified fit
- Clear scope has emerged from mapping
- Client has expressed interest in proceeding

---

## Information Needed Before Starting

**From MAP Session:**
- [ ] Current state documentation
- [ ] Pain points identified (in client's words)
- [ ] Root causes discovered
- [ ] Gaps between current and desired state

**For Solution Design:**
- [ ] Proposed modules/components
- [ ] Scope boundaries (what's in/out)
- [ ] Timeline estimate
- [ ] Resource requirements

**For Pricing:**
- [ ] Total investment amount
- [ ] Payment terms (40/30/30 or 50/50)
- [ ] What triggers each payment

## Complete Proposal Structure

### Section 1: Cover Page

**Elements:**
- TNDS logo (centered, top)
- "Proposal" or "Project Proposal"
- Project name (e.g., "Command Center Build")
- Client company name
- Date
- "Prepared by True North Data Strategies"

**Formatting:**
- Title: Arial, 36pt, Navy
- Client name: Arial, 28pt, Teal
- Date: Arial, 14pt, Black

---

### Section 2: Executive Summary

**Purpose:** One-page summary that could stand alone.

**Length:** 3-4 paragraphs, maximum 1 page

**Structure:**
1. **Opening:** State the problem in one sentence
2. **Context:** What was discovered in MAP session (2-3 sentences)
3. **Solution:** What we propose (2-3 sentences)
4. **Investment:** Price, timeline, and payment terms
5. **Call to action:** Next step

**Example:**

> Peak HVAC has grown to 10 technicians, but the operation hasn't kept up. Jobs disappear into a black hole between dispatch and invoice. The owner works 70-hour weeks and still can't take a day off.
>
> During our MAP session, we identified three root causes: scattered data (texts, paper, spreadsheets), no visibility into job status, and manual invoicing that takes days when it should take minutes.
>
> We propose a Command Center Build that connects ServiceTitan to your operational hub, gives you real-time visibility into every job, and automates invoice creation the moment jobs close.
>
> **Investment:** $4,000 | **Timeline:** 3 weeks | **Payment:** 40% at kickoff, 30% at build complete, 30% at launch.
>
> Review this proposal and let's schedule a call to answer questions and move forward.

---

### Section 3: Current Situation

**Purpose:** Demonstrate understanding of their operation and problems.

**Content:**
- What you observed/learned in MAP session
- Pain points in their words (quote them)
- Root causes of the problems
- Impact of current state (cost, time, stress)

**Structure:**
```
## Current Situation

### What We Found
[2-3 paragraphs summarizing current state]

### The Problems This Creates
- [Problem 1 - in client's voice]
- [Problem 2 - in client's voice]
- [Problem 3 - in client's voice]

### Root Causes
[What's actually driving these problems]
```

**Voice Guidance:**
- Use their words when describing problems
- Be specific (numbers, examples)
- Acknowledge the impact without dramatizing

**Example Content:**

> **What We Found**
>
> Your 10 technicians are productive, but the information about their work doesn't flow to the office until hours—sometimes days—later. Job tickets live on paper in trucks. Status updates come via text messages that get lost in the scroll. When customers call asking about their job, the office has to call the technician to find out.
>
> **The Problems This Creates**
>
> "I don't know where my guys are until they call me."
> "Invoicing takes forever because I'm chasing down job details."
> "I can't take a day off without things falling apart."
>
> **Root Causes**
>
> The core issue isn't your team—it's that your data is scattered across texts, paper tickets, ServiceTitan, and a spreadsheet that nobody updates in real-time. There's no single source of truth for job status, and nothing is automated.

---

### Section 4: Proposed Solution

**Purpose:** Present what you're recommending and why.

**Content:**
- High-level solution description
- How it addresses each root cause
- What components/modules are included
- What the end state looks like

**Structure:**
```
## Proposed Solution

### Overview
[2-3 sentences describing the solution at a high level]

### How It Works
[Explanation of key components]

### What This Changes
[Before → After comparison]
```

**Voice Guidance:**
- Connect every component to a problem
- Use "you'll be able to" language
- Paint the after picture clearly

**Example Content:**

> **Overview**
>
> We'll build a Command Center that connects ServiceTitan to your operational hub, giving you real-time visibility into every job and automating the invoice trigger.
>
> **How It Works**
>
> **data-command** pulls job data from ServiceTitan automatically—no manual entry, no waiting for callbacks. You'll see every technician's status, every job's progress, every issue as it happens.
>
> **financial-command** monitors job completion and triggers invoice creation the moment a job closes. No more chasing paperwork. No more 3-day invoice delays.
>
> **sms-command** sends automatic updates to customers—"your technician is on the way"—without anyone touching a phone.
>
> **What This Changes**
>
> | Before | After |
> |--------|-------|
> | Job status unknown until callback | Real-time visibility into every job |
> | Invoice in 3 days | Invoice same-day |
> | 70-hour weeks | Management by exception |

### Section 5: Scope & Deliverables

**Purpose:** Specify exactly what's included and what's not.

**Content:**
- Detailed list of deliverables
- What's in scope
- What's explicitly out of scope
- Assumptions

**Structure:**
```
## Scope & Deliverables

### What's Included
[Numbered list of deliverables]

### What's Not Included
[List of exclusions]

### Assumptions
[What must be true for this scope to apply]
```

**Voice Guidance:**
- Be specific about deliverables
- Clear boundaries prevent scope creep
- Assumptions protect both parties

**Example Content:**

> **What's Included**
>
> 1. **Command Center Hub** — Central operational dashboard
> 2. **data-command Module** — ServiceTitan integration and data flows
> 3. **financial-command Module** — Invoice automation and job costing
> 4. **sms-command Module** — Customer notification automation
> 5. **Training** — 2-hour training session for office staff
> 6. **Documentation** — User guide and process documentation
> 7. **30-Day Support** — Post-launch support for questions and adjustments
>
> **What's Not Included**
>
> - ServiceTitan configuration or changes
> - Hardware or software licensing fees
> - Data cleanup or migration
> - Additional module development beyond scope
>
> **Assumptions**
>
> - ServiceTitan API access is available
> - One office staff member available for training
> - Client provides timely feedback during build phase

### Section 6: Timeline

**Purpose:** Set expectations for when things happen.

**Content:**
- Total duration
- Phase breakdown
- Key milestones
- What happens each week

**Structure:**
```
## Timeline

**Total Duration:** [X] weeks

### Week-by-Week Plan

**Week 1: [Phase Name]**
- [Activity 1]
- [Activity 2]
- Milestone: [What's done]

**Week 2: [Phase Name]**
[Continue...]

### Key Milestones
| Week | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | [Milestone] |
| 2 | [Milestone] |
```

**Example Content:**

> **Total Duration:** 3 weeks
>
> **Week 1: Setup & Discovery**
> - Kickoff meeting (confirm scope, gather credentials)
> - Connect ServiceTitan API
> - Build data flow foundation
> - Milestone: Data flowing to hub
>
> **Week 2: Build & Configure**
> - Build dashboards and views
> - Configure invoice automation
> - Set up customer notifications
> - Milestone: System functional in test
>
> **Week 3: Launch & Train**
> - Final testing with real data
> - Training session (2 hours)
> - Go-live
> - Milestone: System live, team trained

### Section 7: Investment & Payment Terms

**Purpose:** State the price and how payment works.

**Content:**
- Total investment
- Payment breakdown
- What triggers each payment
- Payment methods accepted

**Structure:**
```
## Investment

**Total Investment:** $[X,XXX]

### Payment Schedule

| Payment | Amount | When |
|---------|--------|------|
| First | $[X,XXX] (XX%) | At kickoff |
| Second | $[X,XXX] (XX%) | At [milestone] |
| Final | $[X,XXX] (XX%) | At launch |

### Payment Methods
[Accepted methods]
```

**Pricing Guidelines:**
- Projects under $4,000: 50/50 payment
- Projects $4,000+: 40/30/30 payment
- Clearly tie payments to milestones

**Example Content:**

> **Total Investment:** $4,000
>
> | Payment | Amount | When |
> |---------|--------|------|
> | First | $1,600 (40%) | At agreement signing |
> | Second | $1,200 (30%) | At build complete (Week 2) |
> | Final | $1,200 (30%) | At go-live (Week 3) |
>
> **Payment Methods:** Check, ACH, Credit Card (3% fee)

### Section 8: Next Steps

**Purpose:** Make the path forward clear.

**Content:**
- What client needs to do
- What happens after they act
- How to ask questions
- Urgency or availability (if applicable)

**Structure:**
```
## Next Steps

1. [Action 1]
2. [Action 2]
3. [Action 3]

**Questions?** [Contact info]
```

**Example Content:**

> 1. **Review this proposal** — Take time to read and consider
> 2. **Let's talk** — Schedule a call to answer questions: [calendar link] or call 719-204-6365
> 3. **Move forward** — Sign the agreement and send first payment to lock in your start date
>
> I have availability to start the week of [date]. Let me know if that works for your team.
>
> **Questions?** Call or text me directly: 719-204-6365

## Proposal Variations

### Command Center Build Proposal

**Emphasis:** Visibility and control
**Key Modules:** data-command, analytics-command, [client-specific]
**Typical Price Range:** $2,500-$5,000
**Typical Timeline:** 2-4 weeks

### Battle Rhythm Install Proposal

**Emphasis:** Consistency and accountability
**Key Deliverables:** Operating cadence, meeting rhythms, accountability systems
**Typical Price Range:** $2,000-$4,000
**Typical Timeline:** 2-4 weeks

### Command Partner Proposal

**Emphasis:** Ongoing support and optimization
**Key Deliverables:** Monthly support hours, system maintenance, optimization
**Typical Price Range:** $1,000-$2,000/month
**Typical Timeline:** Ongoing
**Special Considerations:** Selectivity messaging, value of continuity

## Proposal Quality Checklist

**Content:**
- [ ] Executive summary can stand alone
- [ ] Current situation uses client's words
- [ ] Every proposed component ties to a problem
- [ ] Scope boundaries are clear
- [ ] Timeline is realistic
- [ ] Payment terms are specific

**Voice:**
- [ ] Outcomes-focused language
- [ ] Specific and concrete
- [ ] No jargon
- [ ] Direct and confident

**Formatting:**
- [ ] TNDS branding applied
- [ ] Professional appearance
- [ ] Easy to skim (headers, bullets)
- [ ] Clean page breaks

**Accuracy:**
- [ ] Client name spelled correctly
- [ ] Pricing accurate
- [ ] Dates/timeline accurate
- [ ] Contact info correct

---

## Common Proposal Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Generic problem description | Use client's actual words from MAP |
| Features without benefits | Connect every component to a pain point |
| Vague scope | List specific deliverables, in/out clearly |
| Missing timeline details | Show week-by-week plan |
| Unclear payment terms | Specify amount, timing, and trigger |
