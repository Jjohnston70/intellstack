# Service Agreement Template Guide

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 29, 2026
**Purpose:** Guide for creating service agreements after client accepts proposal

---

## Overview

Service agreements formalize the engagement after a client accepts a proposal. This guide provides the complete structure, section-by-section guidance, and legal considerations.

**Important:** This is a template guide, not legal advice. Have agreements reviewed by legal counsel before use.

---

## When to Create an Agreement

- Client has verbally or in writing accepted the proposal
- Scope, timeline, and pricing are confirmed
- Ready to begin work

---

## Required Information

**Client Information:**
- [ ] Full legal company name
- [ ] Company address
- [ ] Primary contact name and title
- [ ] Contact email and phone

**Engagement Details:**
- [ ] Service type (from proposal)
- [ ] Scope of work (from proposal)
- [ ] Deliverables list
- [ ] Timeline and milestones
- [ ] Total investment
- [ ] Payment schedule

## Complete Agreement Structure

### Section 1: Agreement Header

**Content:**
- Document title: "Service Agreement"
- Between parties (full legal names)
- Effective date
- Agreement reference number (optional)

**Example:**

```
SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE]

BETWEEN:

True North Data Strategies, LLC
Colorado Springs, CO
("Provider")

AND:

[Client Legal Name]
[Client Address]
("Client")
```

---

### Section 2: Services Description

**Purpose:** Define what service is being provided at a high level.

**Content:**
- Service name
- Brief description
- Reference to detailed scope

**Example:**

> **2. Services**
>
> Provider agrees to perform the following services for Client:
>
> **Command Center Build** — Installation of an operational visibility system including data integration, dashboard development, and training as further described in Exhibit A (Scope of Work).

---

### Section 3: Scope of Work

**Purpose:** Detail exactly what's included and excluded.

**Content:**
- Deliverables list
- Specific components/modules
- What's explicitly not included
- Assumptions

**Structure:**

```
3. Scope of Work

3.1 Deliverables
Provider will deliver the following:
a) [Deliverable 1]
b) [Deliverable 2]
c) [Deliverable 3]

3.2 Exclusions
The following are explicitly not included in this engagement:
a) [Exclusion 1]
b) [Exclusion 2]

3.3 Assumptions
This scope assumes:
a) [Assumption 1]
b) [Assumption 2]
```

**Example:**

> **3.1 Deliverables**
>
> Provider will deliver the following:
> a) Command Center Hub — Central operational dashboard
> b) data-command Module — ServiceTitan integration
> c) financial-command Module — Invoice automation
> d) Training — 2-hour session for office staff
> e) Documentation — User guide and process documentation
> f) 30-Day Support — Post-launch questions and adjustments
>
> **3.2 Exclusions**
>
> a) ServiceTitan configuration or account setup
> b) Hardware or third-party software licensing
> c) Data cleanup or historical migration
> d) Development beyond specified modules
>
> **3.3 Assumptions**
>
> a) Client has active ServiceTitan account with API access
> b) Client provides timely feedback during development
> c) One staff member available for training

---

### Section 4: Timeline & Milestones

**Purpose:** Set expectations for delivery schedule.

**Content:**
- Start date
- End date (or duration)
- Key milestones
- Dependencies

**Example:**

> **4. Timeline**
>
> **4.1 Duration:** Three (3) weeks from Effective Date
>
> **4.2 Milestones:**
>
> | Milestone | Target | Trigger |
> |-----------|--------|---------|
> | Kickoff | Week 1, Day 1 | Agreement signed |
> | Build Complete | Week 2, Day 5 | All modules functional |
> | Go-Live | Week 3, Day 5 | Training complete |
>
> **4.3 Dependencies**
> Timeline assumes Client provides necessary access credentials within 2 business days of kickoff and responds to questions within 24 business hours.

---

### Section 5: Payment Terms

**Purpose:** Specify total investment and payment schedule.

**Content:**
- Total amount
- Payment schedule (amounts and timing)
- Payment methods
- Late payment terms

**Example:**

> **5. Payment Terms**
>
> **5.1 Total Investment:** Four Thousand Dollars ($4,000.00 USD)
>
> **5.2 Payment Schedule:**
>
> | Payment | Amount | Due |
> |---------|--------|-----|
> | First | $1,600 (40%) | Upon signing |
> | Second | $1,200 (30%) | At Build Complete |
> | Final | $1,200 (30%) | At Go-Live |
>
> **5.3 Payment Methods:** Check, ACH, or Credit Card (3% processing fee)
>
> **5.4 Late Payment:** Payments not received within 10 days of due date may incur a late fee of 1.5% per month.

---

### Section 6: Deliverables & Acceptance

**Purpose:** Define how deliverables are reviewed and accepted.

**Content:**
- Delivery process
- Review period
- Acceptance criteria
- Revision process

**Example:**

> **6. Deliverables & Acceptance**
>
> **6.1 Delivery:** Provider will deliver each milestone via email or shared documentation.
>
> **6.2 Review Period:** Client has five (5) business days to review each deliverable and provide feedback.
>
> **6.3 Acceptance:** Deliverables are considered accepted if Client does not provide substantive feedback within the review period.
>
> **6.4 Revisions:** Provider will make reasonable revisions to address Client feedback within scope. Requests beyond scope will be addressed via Change Order.

---

### Section 7: Client Responsibilities

**Purpose:** Clarify what the client must provide.

**Content:**
- Access and credentials
- Personnel availability
- Timely feedback
- Decision authority

**Example:**

> **7. Client Responsibilities**
>
> Client agrees to:
> a) Provide access credentials within 2 business days of request
> b) Make one primary contact available for questions
> c) Respond to Provider questions within 24 business hours
> d) Provide timely feedback during review periods
> e) Ensure decision-maker availability for key decisions
>
> Delays caused by Client's failure to meet these responsibilities may extend timeline.

---

### Section 8: Change Orders

**Purpose:** Define how scope changes are handled.

**Content:**
- Change request process
- How changes are priced
- Approval requirements
- Impact on timeline

**Example:**

> **8. Change Orders**
>
> **8.1 Process:** Requests for work beyond the defined scope must be submitted in writing.
>
> **8.2 Pricing:** Provider will respond with a Change Order specifying additional cost and timeline impact.
>
> **8.3 Approval:** No additional work will begin until Change Order is signed by both parties.
>
> **8.4 Timeline Impact:** Change Orders may extend project timeline. Provider will estimate impact in Change Order.

---

### Section 9: Intellectual Property

**Purpose:** Clarify who owns what.

**Content:**
- Client ownership of deliverables
- Provider ownership of methodology
- License for any Provider tools used

**Example:**

> **9. Intellectual Property**
>
> **9.1 Client Ownership:** Upon final payment, Client owns all deliverables created specifically for Client.
>
> **9.2 Provider Methodology:** Provider retains ownership of pre-existing methodologies, frameworks, and tools used in delivery.
>
> **9.3 License:** Client receives perpetual license to use any Provider tools incorporated into deliverables.

---

### Section 10: Confidentiality

**Purpose:** Protect both parties' information.

**Content:**
- Definition of confidential information
- Obligations of both parties
- Exceptions

**Example:**

> **10. Confidentiality**
>
> Each party agrees to keep confidential any proprietary information received from the other party, including but not limited to business processes, financial information, and technical specifications. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.
>
> **Exceptions:** Information that is publicly available, independently developed, or required by law to disclose.

---

### Section 11: Termination

**Purpose:** Define how either party can end the agreement.

**Content:**
- Termination for convenience
- Termination for cause
- Effect of termination (payments, deliverables)

**Example:**

> **11. Termination**
>
> **11.1 For Convenience:** Either party may terminate with 14 days written notice.
>
> **11.2 For Cause:** Either party may terminate immediately if the other party materially breaches and fails to cure within 10 days of notice.
>
> **11.3 Effect:** Upon termination:
> a) Client pays for work completed through termination date
> b) Provider delivers all work completed to that point
> c) Client retains license to use completed deliverables

---

### Section 12: Limitation of Liability

**Purpose:** Limit exposure for both parties.

**Content:**
- Cap on liability
- Exclusion of consequential damages
- Exceptions

**Example:**

> **12. Limitation of Liability**
>
> Provider's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by Client. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.

---

### Section 13: General Provisions

**Purpose:** Standard legal provisions.

**Content:**
- Governing law
- Entire agreement
- Amendment process
- Notice requirements

**Example:**

> **13. General Provisions**
>
> **13.1 Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Colorado.
>
> **13.2 Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.
>
> **13.3 Amendment:** Changes require written agreement signed by both parties.
>
> **13.4 Notice:** All notices shall be sent to the addresses listed above.

---

### Section 14: Signatures

**Purpose:** Formalize agreement.

**Content:**
- Signature lines for both parties
- Print name and title
- Date

**Example:**

```
IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the Effective Date.

PROVIDER:
True North Data Strategies, LLC

_____________________________
Jacob Johnston, Owner
Date: ________________


CLIENT:
[Client Company Name]

_____________________________
[Name], [Title]
Date: ________________
```

## Agreement Variations

### Command Center Build Agreement

**Emphasis:** Deliverables focus on modules and systems
**Typical Scope:** 4-6 specific deliverables
**Typical Payment:** 40/30/30
**Special Terms:** 30-day post-launch support

### Battle Rhythm Install Agreement

**Emphasis:** Process and cadence establishment
**Typical Scope:** Operating cadence, meeting structures, accountability systems
**Typical Payment:** 50/50 or 40/30/30
**Special Terms:** 2-week check-in after launch

### Command Partner Agreement

**Emphasis:** Ongoing support relationship
**Typical Scope:** Monthly hours, defined services, exclusions
**Typical Payment:** Monthly recurring
**Special Terms:** 30-day cancellation notice, annual review
