## Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Core Principles
1. **Direct, No-Fluff Communication**
   - Get to the point immediately
   - No marketing speak or corporate jargon
   - Say what you mean, mean what you say
   
2. **Military-Influenced Clarity**
   - Precise language
   - Clear structure
   - No ambiguity
   - Mission-focused

3. **Confident But Not Arrogant**
   - We know our expertise
   - We don't need to prove it
   - Let the work speak
   - Respectful of client intelligence

4. **Outcomes Over Features**
   - "You'll be able to..." not "This system does..."
   - Focus on client transformation, not tool capabilities
   - Paint the "after" picture clearly

5. **Owner-to-Owner Language**
   - We understand operations, not just tech
   - Speak to business realities
   - Acknowledge the chaos they're living in
   - Position ourselves as fellow operators

### Writing Rules

**DO**:
- Use contractions naturally (don't, won't, can't)
- Write in active voice
- Use specific examples
- Quantify when possible
- Address the reader directly (you, your)
- Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Front-load important information

**DON'T**:
- Use emojis in documentation (ever)
- Use corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, ecosystem)
- Use jargon unless defining technical terms for technical audiences
- Apologize or hedge unnecessarily
- Over-explain simple concepts
- Use passive voice
- Write long, meandering sentences

### Language Patterns

**Client-Facing Tone Examples**:
```
Good: "You'll see every job in real-time, so you know when problems happen—not three days later."
Bad: "Our real-time visibility solution leverages cutting-edge data integration to enhance operational awareness."

Good: "Fixed scope, fixed price. No surprise invoices."
Bad: "We offer transparent pricing models with clearly defined deliverables aligned to your unique requirements."

Good: "If you can't take a day off without things falling apart, we should talk."
Bad: "Organizations experiencing operational dependency challenges may benefit from our solutions."
```

**Internal Document Tone Examples**:
```
Good: "Run IDENTIFY stage. If you get 2+ red flags, walk away."
Bad: "Consider conducting the IDENTIFY phase and evaluating whether the prospect alignment suggests proceeding."

Good: "Call the client. Ask these three questions. Take notes."
Bad: "Initiate client communication to facilitate discovery through structured inquiry methodology."
```

## The Two Protocols (Core Framework)

### The Direction Protocol (Sales Process)

The 5-stage process for taking prospects from chaos to clarity:

```
IDENTIFY → ASSESS → MAP → CHART → LAUNCH
```

**Stage Details**:
1. **IDENTIFY** (15-20 min) - Is there a fit?
   - Initial qualification
   - Pain signal detection
   - Red flag screening
   
2. **ASSESS** (30 min) - Is the problem real?
   - Operational reality check
   - Current state evaluation
   - Stakes and readiness
   
3. **MAP** (90-120 min) - What's broken, what's needed?
   - Deep diagnostic
   - Root cause analysis
   - Solution architecture
   
4. **CHART** (30-45 min) - Here's the course
   - Present findings
   - Deliver proposal
   - Handle objections
   
5. **LAUNCH** - Let's go
   - Execute agreement
   - Kickoff project
   - Begin delivery

**When to Reference**: 
- Always in client-facing service documents
- When explaining prerequisites
- When describing the discovery process

**Key Phrase**: 
> "Direction Protocol gets you clarity. Command Protocol gets you control. That's how we turn data into direction."

**Lists**:
- Bullet style: Standard bullet (•)
- Indentation: 0.25" first line
- Hanging indent: 0.25"
- Spacing: 6pt after each item

- Border: 1pt, Navy (#1a3a5c)

- Too small: < 5 people (not enough complexity)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Voice & Tone Mistakes
❌ **Don't**: "Our cutting-edge solution leverages AI-powered automation to optimize your operational workflows"
✅ **Do**: "You'll see every job in real-time and know when problems happen—not three days later"

❌ **Don't**: "We're passionate about helping you succeed"
✅ **Do**: "We fix operations that are broken"

❌ **Don't**: "Our team of experts will collaborate with your stakeholders"
✅ **Do**: "We'll work with your team to build what you need"

### Structure Mistakes
❌ **Don't**: Bury the important information in the third paragraph
✅ **Do**: Lead with what matters most

❌ **Don't**: Write 8-sentence paragraphs
✅ **Do**: Keep paragraphs to 2-4 sentences

❌ **Don't**: Use bullets for a single item
✅ **Do**: Use bullets for 2+ items, prose for single points

### Formatting Mistakes
❌ **Don't**: Mix fonts (don't add Times New Roman or Georgia)
✅ **Do**: Use Arial throughout

❌ **Don't**: Use random colors
✅ **Do**: Stick to Navy, Teal, Black, Gray

❌ **Don't**: Underline for emphasis
✅ **Do**: Use bold for emphasis

### Content Mistakes
❌ **Don't**: Explain features without outcomes
✅ **Do**: Connect every feature to a client benefit

❌ **Don't**: Use vague language ("improve efficiency")
✅ **Do**: Use specific language ("cut invoice time from 3 days to same-day")

❌ **Don't**: Apologize or hedge ("We try to...", "We hope to...")
✅ **Do**: State confidently ("We build...", "You'll get...")
